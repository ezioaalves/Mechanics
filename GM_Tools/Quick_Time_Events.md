enhance the flavor of new or existing campaigns. Though many of these items are of
vastly different flavors, they should serve to imbue world elements with great flavor in
highly concentrated doses.

SECTION I: NEW TECHNIQUES
This chapter contains 71 new techniques of various styles for characters from all walks of
life. Included among these are four new styles of ninjutsu and taijutsu, each with a brief
history provided for you below.
Ao Higi: Invented by a mad puppeteer whose name is lost to history, this style of
puppetry focuses on power over the true versatility of most puppeteers. It is said that a
puppeteer equipped with the Blue Secret Techniques has the power to bring down whole
empires, like its creator once did.
Haradou: This style has been passed down from priest to acolyte, from monk to
neophyte in temples and monastary for ages, as a tool for meditation and on the path to
enlightenment. In recent years, however, the style has been weaponized to serve as a
defense against the onslaught of demons and the manifestation of true evil. Though many
consider the weaponization of the Way of Purification blasphemous, it has given rise to
many "battle acolytes" in recent years.
Hatsusen Bujutsu: This ancient style is spoken of in tall tales and whispers, and
tells of warriors of prodigious powers borne with the ability to defeat armies and move
mountains with the touch of a finger. While not entirely true, the style of the Eight
Immortals nevertheless favors judicious application of agility and speed over brawn. 
Juuryuu Kenjutsu: Born in the mind of a woman with a passionate, and voracious,
love for battle, the Ten-Ton Style of kenjutsu focuses on absolute earthshaking power
over anyting else. Though it lacks in finesse and doesn't follow many precepts and rituals
typical of martial arts schools, Juuryuu Kenjutsu is regarded by enthusiasts everywhere as
one of the ultimate killing arts.

Ao Higi: Kidan Daidan (Blue Secret Technique: Incineration)
Ninjutsu (requires Ninpou: Chakra no Ito (3) and Advanced Puppetry (a))
Rank: 12 (A-Class); Learn DC: 27, 4 success; Perform requirements: 15 ranks (DC
29); Time: 1 attack action; Components: C, S, E; Range: Medium (20 ft. + 10 ft./2
levels); Area: 20-ft. radius burst; Duration: Instantaneous; Saving Throws: Reflex half
and Fortitude partial (see text); Chakra Cost: 11.
This technique allows a single puppet with the Elemental Cannon, Greater Elemental
Cannon or Epic Elemental Cannon component to a burst attack. This technique requires 4
charges from the elemental scroll and deals 10d6 points of energy damage to all creatures
in the area, halved on a Reflex save. The energy type depends on the elemental scroll
used. Any creature who suffered damage must make a Fortitude save to avoid suffering
additional effects, depending on the element used, if the puppet was using a greater or
epic elemental cannon:
- Earth or Ice: The creature is staggered for 1 round.
- Fire: The target catches on fire.
- Lightning: The target is shaken for 1 round.
- Water or Wind: Target is knocked prone.
The puppet can pay up to 5 points of the chakra cost.
Empower
The user can spend 1 chakra to increase the energy damage by 1 die, up to 1d6 per level
or 18d6.
Material Focus: A puppet with the elemental cannon or greater elemental cannon.

Fuinjutsu
Rank: 6 (B-Class); Learn DC: 20, 3 success; Perform requirements: 8 ranks (DC 21);
Time: 1 attack action; Components: C, M, F (see text); Range: Melee Touch; Target:
One creature or object or personal (see text); Duration: 1 day/level (see text); Saving
Throws: Will negates (see text); Chakra Resistance: Yes (see text); Chakra Cost: 4.
The subject is tagged with a seal that takes the appearance of a circle of symbols, with a
special sealing character appearing in the center when a seeking seal is online.
This technique marks the touched creature with a tagging seal. This creature can be
tracked up to 1 mile by the seeking seal, which must be scribed onto the user. The subject
can make a Will save to resist the seal when it is applied.
The seeking seal can be used to track the target by spending an attack action to channel
minute amounts of chakra into the seal. Doing so displays the direction and exact distance
in feet to the target at the time the information as gathered. If the target was further than 1
mile, only the approximate direction is given, and the seal will display that the target is
out of range
Material Focus: Some ink to scribe the seeking seal.
Scribing the seal requiress a Fuinjutsu check (DC 21), must be done by the user and takes
one seal slot. An attempt to craft the seal takes 2 rounds, and a failed check will result in
the sealing process failing without the user noticing until the end. 
A Fuinjutsu check with the same difficulty class can be attempted to confirm the seal was
crafted successfully (cannot be retried).
Fuinjutsu: Harukami no In (Sealing Technique: Remote Viewing Seal)
Fuinjutsu
Rank: 8 (A-Class); Learn DC: 23, 4 success; Perform requirements: 11 ranks (DC
25); Time: 1 attack action; Components: C, M, F, Mas; Range: 0 feet; Effect: One
viewing or recording seal; Duration: 10 minutes/level (see text); Chakra Cost: 4.
The user places a recording seal on any surface, and a viewing seal on the palm of his
hand. The user can concentrate as an attack action each round by placing the viewing seal
over his closed eye to see and hear through the recording seal as though with a security
camera, although sound is muffled and sight is blurry through the seal, imposing a –10
penalty to Listen and Spot checks made through the seal. 
The recording seal can see up to 60 feet, and glows softly when in use, making easy to see
even in total darkness (Spot check DC 5).
Mastery
Each step of mastery lessens the check penalty by 1.
Material Focus: Some ink to scribe the viewing or recording seal.
Scribing the seal requiress a Fuinjutsu check (DC 25) and must be done by the user. The
viewing seal takes one seal slot. An attempt to craft the seal takes 5 rounds, and a failed

check will result in the sealing process failing without the user noticing until the end. 
A Fuinjutsu check with the same difficulty class can be attempted to confirm the seal was
crafted successfully (cannot be retried).

Ninjutsu [Wind]

with wind bullets; Duration: 1 minute/level or until discharged (D); Saving Throws:
None; Chakra Resistance: Yes; Chakra Cost: 2.
The user imbues his either or both of his hands with wind bullets. Each bullet requires an
attack roll, has a range increment of 50 feet and deals 2d6 wind damage, but otherwise

This technique creates 2 bullets, which must be distributed between the hands the user

Mastery
With the third and fifth steps of mastery, an additional wind bullet is generated when
using this technique.

Fuuton: Taifuu Kekkai (Wind Style: Cyclone Barrier)
Ninjutsu (Fuuton) [Wind]
Rank: 12 (A-Class); Learn DC: 27, 4 success; Perform requirements: 15 ranks (DC
29); Time: 1 attack action; Components: C, H, E; Range: Close (10 ft. + 5 ft./2 levels);
Area: Hemisphere of wind 5-ft. radius per two levels, 20-ft. high; Duration:
Concentration (up to 1 round/level) plus 3 rounds (D); Saving Throws: Reflex half (see

text); Chakra Resistance: Yes; Chakra Cost: 14.
The user creates a cyclone of powerful wind sickles. Any creature or object that passes
through the barrier suffers 10d6 points of wind damage, halved on a successful Reflex
save. Any creature in the area where the edge of the barrier would appear takes damage as
though going through the barrier, but can avoid the wall and take no damage on a
successful save, ending up on the side of its choice.
The cyclone barrier provides one-half cover against attacks made through it, and any
projectiles such as arrows and thrown weapons have a 20% miss chance on attacks made
through the barrier, but ballistic weapons are unaffected. Gases, most gaseous breath
weapons, and creatures in gaseous form cannot pass through the wall (although
incorporeal creatures are unaffected).
Empower
The user can spend 1 chakra to increase the damage dealt by the barrier by 1d6,
maximum 1d6 per level or 16d6.

Fuuton: Egukiri (Wind Style: Excavation Drill)
Ninjutsu (Fuuton; requires Ninpou: Chakra Nagashi (3)) [Armed, Hijutsu, Wind]
Rank: 8 (B-Class); Learn DC: 22, 4 success; Perform requirements: 10 ranks (DC
23); Time: 1 attack action; Components: S, E, F; Range: 0 feet; Effect: Turns the
weapon into a wind drill; Duration: 1 round/level or until discharged (D); Saving
Throws: None; Chakra Resistance: Yes; Chakra Cost: 5.

The user imbues his slicing weapon with more chakra. A split instant before being
activated, the power in the weapon surges to form a huge conical drill that ravages the
enemy.
This technique can only be used on a chakra current weapon currently imbued with the
Slicing effect. The weapon is imbued with a wind drill that can be activated as a swift
action immediately before making an attack. The wind drill is discharged whether or not
the attack hits, and uses two attacks of the Slicing effect. If the slicing effect had 2 or less
attacks, it is consumed.
Attacking with the wind drill is a touch attack made with the weapon and deals weapon
damage plus 4d6 as half wind, half piercing damage, and can be done as an attack action
or as part of a full-attack action. The weapon gains increased reach by 5 feet when
attacking with the wind drill, but can still target adjacent enemies. The damage ignores all
hardness and damage reduction, but not energy resistance and is still subject to chakra
resistance.
Empower
The user can pay 1 chakra to increase the damage by one die, maximum one-half his level
plus 2 or 12d6.
The user can pay 4 chakra to increase the size of the drill just before the attack. The wind
drill attack allows the user to make a free attack against a secondary target in the square
behind the target, relative to his own position. The attack is made at a -4 penalty and
deals half damage.
Mastery
The fifth step of mastery does allows the technique to be used without the Slicing effect
already active and applies the effect on the weapon for the next two successful attacks
after activating the drill or 1 minute.
Material Focus: A chakra current weapon.

Collaboration Techniques
A collaboration effect is a type of technique that is achieved when two or more
participants perform two or more component techniques simultaneously. The
collaboration effect must be learned beforehand, following the normal rules for learning a
technique. All component techniques must be performed during the same initiative count,
which is typically accomplished by delaying one's action, and a character can only
perform 1 collaboration effect per encounter.
The collaboration effect overrides the effects of all component techniques when
performed, unless specified otherwise, and uses the highest saving throw amongst the
component techniques as the basis for all required saves, plus a bonus to the saving throw
equal to one-half the collaboration effect's rank (round down). In addition, the ranks of
collaboration effects range from 1 to 6 rather than 1 to 14. Learning a collaboration effect
is a Learn check modified only by the character's Intelligence modifier. 
There are no subtypes for collaboration effects. Furthermore, the perform time of the a
collaboration effect may vary depending on the perform time of the component
techniques, and it is not increased. Each different character involved in a collaboration
effect is referred to as a participant, and any variant that calls for level refers to the
participant levels, which is obtained by using the average value of all participants
involved in the collaboration effect (mastery may increase the effective level of one of the
participants to determine the maximum values of the technique). 
Furthermore, collaboration effects have no additional chakra cost outside of the
component techniques, and any collaboration effect that deals varying damage will deal
damage based on the total chakra spent by all participants to create the collaboration
effect. Any class ability, such as the elementalist's elemental fury, or meta-chakra feat
applied to the component techniques by all participants also apply to all effects and
damage dealt by the collaboration effect, but the collaboration effect itself cannot be
modified. However, multiple instances of the same abilities or feat do not stack, use the
strongest instead (for example, two different instances of the Elemental Fury ability
applying to damage types found in the collaboration effect would only apply once).

Rank: A collaboration technique's rank ranges from 1 to 6, unlike normal techniques. To
offset this, learning a collaboration technique requires an extra success in addition to the
number of successes required by its complexity rating.
Learning: A collaboration technique is learned like a normal technique, but a character
learning a collaboration technique on his own, without a partner learning the same
technique at the same time or to teach him that technique takes a –5 penalty to Learn
checks.
Mastery: A collaboration technique isn't affected by mastery of the collaboration
technique itself normally. However, a fully mastered collaboration technique can be used
once per encounter without counting towards the maximum number of times a character
can use collaboration techniques.
Technique Description: A new entry appears in the technique description of
collaboration effects: Requirements. This list the requirements one must meet in order to
learn a collaboration effect.
Components: Some component techniques may have additional components, as
described below.
- Adjacent (A): All participants must be in adjacent squares to perform the technique.
- Flanking (K): All participants must be in a position to flank the opponents.
- Combined Damage (D): The technique deals combined damage of at least two types of
damage; if a creature is vulnerable to any damage types, both all types deal increased
damage. If the creature is immune to any damage type, the effect deals damage and
removes the portion of damage that would be ignored from the total as any remaining
damage type; if the creature is immune to all types, the attack has no effect. Energy
resistance applies normally.

Alter Gravity: You can change the gravity inside the shard, either to increase, decrease

or remove it entirely. The effects of increased or decreased gravity are as follow.
High Gravity: The gravity inside the shard is much more intense than the real world. All
characters inside the shard suffer a –2 penalty to Balance, Climb, Jump, Ride, Swim and
Tumble checks, as well as attack rolls.
The weight of any carried item is doubled, and the range increment of any weapon is
halved (round down. A character that falls in a high gravity shard suffers 1d10 points of
damage for every 10 feet fallen, maximum 20d10.
Ultra High Gravity: As with high gravity shards, ultra high gravity imposes a –5 penalty
to checks and attack rolls, and the weight of carried items is quadrupled. The damage
taken from falls increases to 1d12 every 10 feet fallen, maximum 20d12.
Low Gravity: The gravity in the shard is far lesser than in the real world. All characters
inside a low gravity shard suffer a –2 penalty to Balance, Ride, Swim and Tumble checks,
as well as weapon damage rolls. 
The weight of carried items is halved, and the range increment of weapons is doubled.
The damage from falls in a low gravity shard is 1d4 for every 10 feet fallen, maximum
20d4.
No Gravity: The characters inside a no gravity shard simply float in place, unless they
have a way to move or a specific mechanism is in place to provide movement.
Alter Time: You can alter the way the time flows inside the shard relative to the real
world, as follows.
Fast: When the flow of time is increased inside a shard, time passes more slowly on the
outside. For example, if time is 50% quicker inside a shard, every minute (10 rounds)
spent inside is 1 minute and 30 seconds on the outside (15 rounds).
Slow: However, when time is slowed inside a shard, it passes more quickly on the
outside. Every minute (10 rounds) spent inside a shard with time 50% slower is roughly
42 seconds (7 rounds) on the ourside.
Elemental Affinity: You can key a shard to encourage a particular elemental affinity, or
to inhibit it. The affinity or inhibition must be selected from the following list: earth, fire,
holy, ice, lightning, unholy, water, or wind.
Minor Affinity: A minor affinity increases the effective character level or hit die of any
spell, power, technique and spell-like ability with the appropriate descriptor by 1.
Greater Affinity: A greater affinity has the same effects as a minor affinity, but also
increases the damage dealt by such effects by one-half (+50%).
Minor Inhibition: A minor inhibition decreases the effective character level or hit die of
any spell, power, technique and spell-like ability with the appropriate descriptor by 1.
Greater Inhibition: A greater inhibition has the same effects as a minor inhibition, but
also decreases the damage dealt by such effects by one-half (-50%).

Quick-Time Events
A quick time event is a scripted skill challenge within an encounter, always initiated by a
NPC. As a general rule, the outcome of each quick-time event (QTE) is left at the GM's
discretion, but there are two types of QTEs: scripted and open-ended. Regardless of the
type, however, all quick-time events require some foresight and a great deal of planning.
Open-Ended Quick-Time Event
This quick-time event is normally initiated by a NPC, but not always. While the player
has no control over the outcome of a successful check, an open-ended QTE generally
proceeds as a back-and-forth between the NPC and the player, and the player is able to
decide the way in which he or she will react. For example, an open-ended QTE generally
begins with the initiator attacking and the target (or targets) defending. During the first
stage, the defenders can decide the way in which they respond to an attack from a few
available choices (Reflex or Fortitude saving throws, ability check, etc.), with a difficulty
set by the GM according to how effective a certain mode of defense is against that
particular attack. Likewise, when the player is the attacker, he may choose his mode of
attack and have a similarly varying degree of success, depending on the difficulty set by
the GM.
Scripted Quick-Time Event
A scripted quick-time event is one where the GM sets the outcome of each success and
failure and sets the skill challenge himself, leavnig the player with only one option. While
this may appear restrictive at first, the scripted QTE makes for tense, rapidfire exchanges
that can spice up combat significantly. However, a scripted QTE requires more
preparation than the open-ended QTE.
Creating a Quick-Time Event
The creation of a QTE can be as little or as much work as the GM wishes it to be. Each
event consists of multiple steps, generally no more than five; each step consisting of
either an opposed skill challenge or a check against a set difficulty. It is important to note
that while a QTE has a real effect on a combat, it is purely cinematic; neither party
expend the use of any abilities, feats or items during a quick-time event, despite what the
flavor of a particular event might lead one to believe. The GM is encouraged to stray
from the normal rules and into the realm of fantasy to up the ante even further, and shed
the constraints of realism for the freedom of imagination.

As previously stated, the outcome of each quick-time event is left entirely at the GM's
discretion, but there are a few guidelines to keep in mind. Regardless of the outcome, the
player should not be made aware of any specifics concerning the NPC's condition or the
victory track (see below), unless they are plainly obvious.
Failure Threshold: The failure threshold indicates the number of steps that a player is
allowed to fail in a row before the quick-time event comes to an end. This mechanic
comes into play when the number of failed challenges reaches the failure threshold, and
an additional penalty is typically imposed upon the player for doing so. The NPC is not
subject to the failure threshold.
Success: A successful challenge brings a player one step closer to success. In general,
there are two ways to go about this: an effect predetermined by the GM occurs (a set
amount of damage is dealt, the NPC gains a persistent condition, etc.), or the player
moves one step forward on a victory track, or both. If more than one player is involved in
the QTE, the challenge is considered a success if at least half of the involved parties
succeed.
Failure: A failed challenge generally results in the player taking a certain percentage of
his maximum hit points in damage or, in more extreme cases, gaining a persistent
condition for a certain number of rounds, or the duration of the encounter. If more than
one player is involved in the QTE, each player suffers the effect of his or her individual
failures, not the whole group's.
Victory Track
In an encounter where multiple QTEs will be initiated, it can be helpful to keep track of
progress simply by using a victory track. Each time a player beats a challenge, he moves
forward on the victory track by one step. The victory track should have as many or as few
steps as the GM wishes, and the cumulative success of all players involved in QTEs
throughout the encounter should count towards moving along the victory track.
A victory track, for example, could look like this: –1 penalty to armor class > –1 penalty
to armor class, –1 penalty to saves > –2 penalty to armor class and saves > –2 penalty to
armor class and saves, –2 penalty to attack rolls > –3 penalty to armor class, attack rolls
and saves > –4 penalty to armor class, attack rolls and saves > –5 penalty to armor class,
attack rolls and saves > –5 penalty to armor class, attack rolls and saves, movement speed
is halved > –10 penalty to armor class, attack rolls and saves, movement speed is halved.
The above victory track has 9 steps, meaning that no more than 10 to 12 challenges
should be allowed throughout the encouner.
Example Scripted Quick-Time Event
The example below features Kazuma, a samurai, facing off against his nemesis, Shiguro
(200 hp), a ruthless blackguard.
Quick-time Event: Charge of the Necromant (5 challenges, failure threshold 2)
Failure Threshold: Kazuma is stunned for 1 round.
Victory Track: –1 penalty to armor class and saves > –1 penalty to armor class, attack
rolls, skill checks and saves > –2 penalty to armor class, attack rolls, skill checks and

saves > –5 penalty to armor class, attack rolls, skill checks and saves.
– Challenge 1:  DC 20 Reflex save. Shiguro charges forward, sword drawn and alight
with a vibrant purple energy! His strike comes from up high and brings with it a surge of
fel power that threatens to split the battlefield in two!
Success: Shiguro takes 1 damage, +1 step on victory track. Kazuma rolls out of the way
and to his feet in an instant, hand ablaze with a retaliatory strike of his own! A ray of
bright orange spellfire hits Shiguro square in the chest with a satisfying sound of impact.
Failure: Kazuma takes 10% max hp damage. Kazuma fails to come up with an entirely
successful defense! Though he manages to avoid the worst of the attack, his shoulder is
caught in the attack and he is sent staggering back, reeling from the blow! He picks
himself up off the ground with some difficulty.
– Challenge 2:  DC 25 Concentration check. The blackguard presses the attack with
renewed fervor! He shouts a word of power that sends a shockwave straight through
Kazuma's body, and just as he prepares to make his strike, skeletal alabaster hands shoot
out of the ground and begin to claw at his legs!
Success: +1 step on victory track. Kazuma is not deterred by the attempt! With only a
murmured word and a hastily-formed thought, he conjures a force shield in his left hand
while a blade of pure fire appears in his right! He blocks Shiguro's strike and makes
quick work of the grasping skeletons!
Failure: Kazuma takes 10% max hp damage and pushed back 10 feet. Kazuma manages
to conjure a quick shield despite the grasping hands, but it proves to be too weak!
Shiguro's sword impacts it, and with a great effort, shatters it to pieces! Kazuma is cut a
second time and forced back!
– Challenge 3:  DC 30 Attack roll. The battle sorcerer moves to the offensive! A curved
sword flies out of its sheath and comes alight with sparks with a spoken word as Kazuma
swings it at Shiguro's neck with all his might!
Success: Shiguro takes 20 damage. The attack strikes true, but Kazuma fails to follow
through entirely when the blackguard catches the sword in his iron-clad hands! 
Failure: Kazuma takes 10% max hp damage. Shiguro nudges the attack aside with one
of his pauldrons and comes in low! Kazuma isn't quick enough to avoid a deep cut to his
belly!
– Challenge 4:  DC 15 Constitution check A furious exchange of blows ensues, as both
sides exchange as many cuts and stabs and slashes as the other dodges! The room is
quickly filled with the sound of steel striking steel, and resonating basso booms as waves
of bright orange and vibrant purple power collide!
Success: Shiguro takes 10 damage, +1 step on victory track. The battle sorcerer focus
doesn't waver even for a moment during the exchange. Soon, the heavily-armored
blackguard begins to falter, his focus slipping only long enough for Kazuma to press the
advantage. Shiguro's sword is batted aside in time for Kazuma's off-hand to come up to
his enemy's torso and spill forth a massive torrent of flames!
Failure: Kazuma takes 10% max hp damage. Kazuma easily keeps ahead during the
exchange, trading furious blows with his slower opponent and taking satisfaction as the
latter begins to falter! But his assault cost him, and he couldn't keep up the pace for long
before he slipped and stumbled head-first into a blast of purple spellfire.

– Challenge 5:  DC 20 Reflex save. Shiguro growls in frustration, and declares that he
will put an end to this ridiculous contest. He brandishes his sword, which again begins to
burn with purple energy, and charges right back into the fray!
Success: Shiguro takes 10 damage, +1 step on victory track. Kazuma decides to meet his
opponent head-on and infuses his own sword with power. Shiguro's greater reach proves
to be a great advantage, however, until Kazuma dives into a roll at the last second and
he scores a cut on his opponent's side, even as both sides of the room begin to collapse in
the thunderous aftershock of the attack.
Failure: Kazuma is disarmed, his weapon lands 1d6x5 feet away. Kazuma decides to
meet his opponent head-on and infuses his own sword with power. Shiguro's greater
reach proves to be a too much of an advantage, however, as even when Kazuma dives
into a roll to slip under his guard, the unholy weapon still continues its deadly descent,
and it is all the sorcerer can do to deflect the blow, his sword wrenched from his grip.
