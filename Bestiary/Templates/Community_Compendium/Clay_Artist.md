CLAY ARTIST (TEMPLATE)
A creature is considered a “clay artist” after performing
the kinjutsu technique Kuchi Sakusei no Jutsu that
creates mouths on the body in order to use Kibaku
Nendo techniques. Such transformations include two
mouths on the palm of each hand and one on the user’s
chest. Withdrawing a bit of clay from his pouch, one of
the mouths can consume the clay, imbue it with his
Explosive chakra and fashion it into a creature. The user
will retain control over the item and can decide what
actions the clay art will take.
Skills: Craft (Visual Arts) is always a class skill.
Special Qualities: Kibaku Nendo Mouths.
Effective Character Level Adjustment: +1.
Challenge Rating: +0.
EXPLOSION RELEASE
Kibaku Nendo techniques are a combination of
Explosion Release as well as Earth Release. The Fire and
Lightning natures make up the Explosion Release but the
manipulation of the clay utilizes Earth Release. In order
to benefit from this template the user needs Explosion
Release and Earth Release, without these the user will
not be able to use the Kibaku Nendo techniques.
NINJA CLAY
The Clay Artist develops the ability to use Earth
Release to manipulate rock and water within the earth
to create special clay utilized in Kibaku Nendo
techniques. This clay unless properly stored (see Clay
Storage Pouch sealed item), will dry out and be rendered
useless. If ninja clay is left out at in open air or even
enclosed but at temperatures beyond a range of 60 to 70
degrees Fahrenheit, the clay will begin to dry out at a
rate of 1 pound an hour.

KIBAKU NENDO MOUTHS
The user has two mouths on the palm of each hand.
The mouths allow the user to Perform Kibaku Nendo
techniques, by consuming the clay, imbuing it with
Explosion Release and fashioning it into a creature by
using Earth Release. As a part of the casting time of each
technique, the user must succeed in a Craft (Visual Arts)
check, with DC equal to the Perform DC of the
technique. Failure means that the sculpture is defective,
and won’t work properly. If the check fails roll a d6 and
look at the table below.
Result (1d6):
1-2: The sculpture has one or more defective
limb/wing/appendage, its speed reduced to 10 ft.
3-4: The sculpture seems to work just fine, but will
explode regardless of the user’s will, after 1d3 rounds.
5-6: The sculpture seems to work just fine, but won’t
explode when commanded, and when destroyed it goes
up in a harmless puff of smoke.
The sculptures can take upon any form, from a bird to
a spider, and always carries a nasty surprise in explosive
form. To create the sculpture, the user needs 3 pounds of
clay per size category above Tiny (For example, a large
sculpture would need 12 pounds of clay). After chewing
and spiting the sculpture back from one of the mouths
on the user (including their original mouth) and making
the necessary hand seals, the sculpture will be enveloped
by a puff of smoke and will increase to the appropriate
size. The maximum size and amount of clay used
depends on the technique used (Kibaku Nendo – C1,
Kibaku Nendo – C2, etc). The statistics of each sculpture
are given below:
Diminutive (1 pounds): A diminutive sculpture must
occupy the square of a creature to deal any damage, as it
deals up to 5d8 point of explosion damage in a 5-ft. radius
burst. The diminutive sculpture has a +12 bonus to Hide
and Move Silently checks, a Chakra Pool equal to 2 times
the number of die it carries in its explosion, and a
defense of 19 and 1 hit point.
Tiny (3 pounds): A tiny sculpture must occupy the
square of a creature to deal any damage, as it deals up to
7d8 point of explosion damage in a 5-ft. radius burst. The
tiny sculpture has a +8 bonus to Hide and Move Silently
checks, a Chakra Pool equal to 2 times the number of die
it carries in its explosion, and a defense of 17 and 6 hit
points.
Small (6 pounds): A small sculpture can carry up to 50
pounds of material and may deal up to 9d8 point of
explosion damage in a 10-ft.-radius burst. It has a +9
bonus to Hide and Move Silently checks, a chakra pool
equal to 2 times the number of die it carries in its
explosion, and a defense of 15 and 9 hit points.
Medium-sized (9 pounds): A Medium-sized sculpture
can carry up to 300 pounds of material and may deal up
to 11d8 point of explosion damage in a 20-ft-radius
burst. It has a +4 bonus to Hide and Move Silently
checks, a chakra pool equal to 2 times the number of die
it carries in its explosion, and a defense of 13 and 17 hit
points.
Large-sized (12 pounds): A Large-sized sculpture can
carry up to 500 pounds of material and may deal up to
13d8 point of explosion damage in a 30-ft-radius burst.
It has a +4 bonus to Hide and Move Silently checks, a
chakra pool equal to 2 times the number of die it carries
in its explosion, and a defense of 11 and 30 hit points.
Huge-sized (15 pounds): A Huge-sized sculpture can
carry up to 1000 pounds of material and may deal up to
15d8 point of explosion damage in a 40-ft-radius burst.
It has a +0 bonus to Hide and Move Silently checks, a
chakra pool equal to 2 times the number of die it carries
in its explosion, and a defense of 9 and 50 hit points.
Any size listed by an Kibaku Nendo technique is the
maximum size category of sculpture that technique can
create.
The sculpture moves at a speed of 30 feet per round
and acts on the user's mental directions only. It cannot
be taken over or affected by any mind-affecting effects,
and is immune to all conditions, as well as sneak attacks
and critical hits.
The user can spend 2 times the required amount of
clay and 2 extra points of chakra per sculpture, and
create a flying sculpture that can move at a flying speed
of 40 feet (average). The DC of the Craft check is
increased by 5.
When creating medium-sized or smaller sculptures,
the user may choose to create a swarm of smaller
creatures (instead of a single, bigger creature) by
spending two extra points of chakra per additional
sculpture the user wishes to make. The DC of the Craft
check is increased by 5.
By adding faster legs or a second pair of wings, the user
can spend 2 times the required amount of clay and 2
extra points of chakra per sculpture to double the speed
of his sculptures. The DC of the Craft check is increased
by 10.
DC increases are cumulative. So, if the user wants to
create a swarm of smaller creatures with faster legs, the
DC would be increased by 10 and the chakra cost would
be increased by 4.
The user may set off the explosion of any sculpture, as
long as it remains within sight, as a move action, which
sets off a number of sculptures equal to the user’s
Intelligence modifier. Once destroyed, when the
duration expires or when it receives more electrical
damage than one third of its current hit points, the
sculpture reverts back to inanimate, harmless clay.
The user may control a number of sculptures equal to
half their character level + their Intelligence modifier at
one time. Attempting to create more to control renders
previous ones useless and harmless.

This technique has two variations:
C2 Dragon: Following the same rules as Kibaku
Nendo: C1 no Bakuhatsu, but the user creates a huge
sized flying sculpture (see Clay Artist for details) that

action, the dragon will draw in some of its tail to create
numerous spheres (1 sphere/creator’s level), which it
will expel from its mouth. The spheres are land mines
that disguise themselves with the terrain they rest upon
(DC 20 Spot), which deal half the damage the dragon
would, according to how much chakra the user charged

spheres can be kept harmless if the creator desires, and
won’t explode until the user activates them (a move
action). The huge flying sculpture and the land mines
remain active for 1 minute/level. The chakra cost to use
C2 Dragon is 3 points of chakra for every d8 the user
wants to charge the sculpture with. Using this version of
the technique takes a full-round action.
Flying Dragon: Following the same rules as Kibaku
Nendo: C1 no Bakuhatsu the user creates a huge flying
sculpture that resembles a dragon. This version lasts for
1 round/level and consumes an attack action to use. The
chakra cost is the same as Kibaku Nendo: C1 no

KIBAKU NENDO: C4 KARURA (EXPLODING CLAY: C4 GARUDA)
Ninjutsu (Bakuton) (Requires Kibaku Nendo (5), Kibaku
Nendo: C1 no Bakuhatsu (5), Kibaku Nendo: C3 no
Bakuhatsu (3)) and Kibaku Nendo Mouths (a))
[Explosion, Kinjutsu]
Rank: 16 (Epic); Learn DC: 46, 7 successes; Perform: 19
(DC 46); Time: 1 Full-round action; Components: C, S;
Range: 5 feet; Effect: 20 feet radius burst/cloud;
Duration: 5 Rounds; Save: Fortitude partial (See text);
Cost: 40.
This is the deadliest form of the Kibaku Nendo
technique. First, the user ingests clay using his real
mouth. Then, the expelled clay will combine to form a
huge version of the user. After forming the needed hand
seals, the giant version of the user will explode into a 20
ft. burst of fine microscopic clay bombs. These tiny
objects will form a cloud, and be controlled by the user'
as they move 30 ft. per round towards any direction the
user wishes. The victim will then unknowingly breathe
in the cloud. These tiny objects will then travel
throughout the body and detonate, causing microscopic
explosions. This in turn causes the body to disintegrate
on a cellular level.
Any living creature that breathes the deadly cloud
must succeed in a fortitude saving throw 1d3+1 rounds
later. Failure means that the victim dies immediately,
while success means that the creature breathed only part
of the cloud, receiving 1d6 points of Strength, Dexterity
and Constitution damage, as his body is torn apart from
inside out. The cloud is invisible, and can’t be seen unless
the user can See Chakra or See Through Chakra. The
target may still negate its effects by receiving at least 15
points of lightning damage to their own body before
they are required to make the afore mentioned saving
throw, but must properly identify this technique
beforehand. Differently from most Kibaku Nendo
techniques, moving the cloud each round requires
concentration (otherwise it will remain on the same
place until the duration expires).
Expendable Components – 15 pounds of clay.

cost of 1 point of chakra every round. The character
gains a bonus to unarmed damage or damage reduction
equal to the specified number. The user can switch the
function of this ability as a swift action once per round.
This ability can be deactivated as a free action.
