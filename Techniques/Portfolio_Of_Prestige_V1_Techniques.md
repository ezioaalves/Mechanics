# Portfolio of Prestige Volume I Techniques

## Fujin no Waza (Battle Formation)
**Genjutsu** (Requires 3 or more Grand Scholar discoveries (a)) [Lost Hijutsu]
**Rank:** 12 (S-Class); **Learn DC:** 28, 6 success; **Perform requirements:** 16 ranks (DC 32); **Time:** 1 round; **Components:** C, H, E; **Range:** Medium (20 ft. + 10 ft./2 levels); **Target:** One creature, and up to 2 other allies (see text); **Duration:** Concentration, or up to 3 rounds or until expended (see text); **Saving Throws:** Will negates (see text); **Chakra Resistance:** Yes; **Chakra Cost:** 5.

As part of performing this technique, the user carefully studies the movement of an opponent in combat. The user first makes a Knowledge check to identify its strength and weaknesses (DC 15+the opponent's Hit Dice). A successful check means the opponent is identified and must save against the technique. If the opponent fails its save, the technique proceeds.
The user gains 3 points to assign to the opponent, divided however he wishes between attack points, damage points and vulnerability points. These points can then be used by up to 2 allies each time they make an attack or perform a technique against the opponent, to various effects. When a point is targeted, it provides a bonus only once and then vanishes.
*   **Attack point:** Attacks made against these points gain a +2 circumstance bonus to attack rolls.
*   **Damage point:** Attacks made against damage points deal an additional 1d4 points of damage, not multiplied on a critical hit.
*   **Vulnerability point:** Techniques targetting vulnerability points have a saving throw difficulty increased 1 point.
The points appear as vague halos around specific areas noted by the user when the technique was performed. As long as the technique lasts, any ally that can target the noted points gain a +4 bonus to Spot checks made against the creature, and negates partial concealment provided by darkness, mist or smoke. For any creature that can see chakra or see through chakra and also see the marked points, the halos negate total concealment as well.
Because the opponent constantly adapts to battle, the duration of the technique is short-lived and the user must make a Concentration check (DC 25) to maintain concentration on the technique if he ever loses line of sight on the subject. This technique can only be used on the same creature once per day.

**Empower**
The user can spend 1 chakra to increase the number of affected allies by 2, up to 1 ally per level.
The user can spend 2 chakra to increase the number of points to assign by 1, up to one-half his character level.
**Mastery**
The first and fifth step of mastery increase the maximum number of rounds the user can concentrate on the technique by 1.
The second step of mastery increases the additional damage of damage points to 1d6.
The third step of mastery increases the bonus to attack rolls of attack points to +3.
The fourth step of mastery in this technique also increases the effective character level of techniques performed against vulnerability points by 1.

## Fuinjutsu: Kinjitou (Sealing Technique: Instant Masterwork)
**Fuinjutsu** (Requires Master at work (a) and either Craft Armor Seals (f) or Craft Weapon Seals (f)) [Lost Hijutsu]
**Rank:** 8 (A-Class); **Learn DC:** 23, 5 success; **Perform requirements:** 11 ranks (DC 25); **Time:** 1 attack action; **Components:** C, H, X; **Range:** 0 feet; **Effect:** Empowers one weapon or armor; **Duration:** 1 round/level (see text); **Chakra Cost:** See text.

The user scribes on the chosen weapon or suit of armor a temporary enhancement seal, so long as the weapon would be able to receive an additional seal slot or enhancement seal and the user would normally be able to craft the seal. The technique has a chakra cost equal to the level of the chosen seal plus 3.
The difficulty of crafting the seal is equal DC 10+the level of the chosen seal. If the chosen weapon or suit of armor already has an enhancement seal slot that would accomodate the enhancement seal, scribing the seal only takes 1 round and the technique's duration is in minutes rather than rounds.
**Expendable Components:** Materials to scribe a seal and a special ink blend worth wealth check DC 10. Scribing the seal requiress a Fuinjutsu check (as shown above), does not have to be done by the user and takes one seal slot on the chosen piece of equipment. An attempt to craft the seal takes 3 rounds, and a failed check will result in the sealing process failing without the user noticing until the end. 
A Fuinjutsu check with the same difficulty class can be attempted to confirm the seal was crafted successfully (cannot be retried).

## Juuka: Douhatsu Shouten (Animal State: All-consuming Rage)
**Taijutsu** (Enhancement; requires Rage (a) or Frenzy (a))
**Rank:** 10 (A-Class); **Learn DC:** 25, 4 success; **Perform requirements:** 13 ranks (DC 27); **Time:** 1 swift action; **Components:** C, P; **Range:** Personal; **Target:** You; **Duration:** 1 round; **Chakra Cost:** 12.

Chakra courses through the user's body and amplifies the power of rage tenfold.
This technique can only be used while under the effects of a rage or frenzy effect with at least 2 rounds remaining to the effect. For the duration of the technique, the character gains damage reduction 5/— and deals an additional 2d6 points of damage with any melee attack, but suffers an additional -2 penalty to Defense. 
After the technique ends, all rage and frenzy effect on the user end as well. The user becomes exhausted for 1d4 rounds and then fatigued for the duration of the encounter.

## Taijutsu: Arakure Daidan (Hand-to-Hand: Sudden Attack)
**Taijutsu** (Strike)
**Rank:** 4 (C-Class); **Learn DC:** 17, 2 success; **Perform requirements:** 5 ranks (DC 17); **Time:** 1 attack action; **Components:** M; **Range:** Melee Attack; **Target:** One creature; **Duration:** Instantaneous; **Chakra Cost:** 3.

The user delivers a powerful attack with an improvised weapon. If the attack hits, the improvised weapon immediately breaks beyond repair but the attack deals damage as though the weapon was two size category larger. If the user has the improvised weapons class feature, the attack also threatens a critical hit on a natural roll of 18, 19 or 20.

## Taijutsu: Nagewaza - Ohage Dachi (Hand-to-Hand: Throw Technique - Takedown Stance)
**Taijutsu** (Stance; requires Combat Throw (f))
**Rank:** 6 (B-Class); **Learn DC:** 20, 3 success; **Perform requirements:** 8 ranks (DC 21); **Time:** 1 swift action; **Components:** M; **Range:** Personal; **Target:** You; **Duration:** Stance; **Chakra Cost:** 6.

While in this stance, the user gains a +2 competence bonus on attack rolls and Grapple checks to establish and maintain a grapple, and a +2 bonus to damage when using making a Grapple check to deal damage. In addition, whenever the user performs a Taijutsu: Nagewaza technique or a Taijutsu: Juuryuu technique, he gains a +2 bonus to all damage dealt by those techniques as well. If the user has the deadly hold class feature, he may apply the deadly hold damage instead of gaining a +2 bonus to damage.
