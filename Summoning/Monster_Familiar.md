# Monster Familiar

A character can enter in a contract with a monster familiar by permanently improving a creature's attitude to **Friendly** or better, and completing a lengthy, expensive and difficult ritual to bind it to himself in a way not unlike a summoner's blood pact.

Despite the name, the monster familiar doesn't have to possess monstrous traits or inhuman qualities, but it cannot be of a race or creature type that doesn't have at least one racial hit die.

Only a character who hasn't signed a blood pact can acquire a monster ritual. Furthermore, a character with a monster familiar can never, under normal circumstances, sign a blood pact. At the GM's discretion, the character may be able to undergo a quest or ritual to undo the effects of the binding and thus gain a blood pact, but must do so at the cost of his bond with the monster familiar. Regardless of the ritual, a character can never have both a blood pact and a monster familiar.

## The Ritual
The process of crafting the bonding ritual is almost as important as completing it, and must be perfectly tailored to the character and his chosen candidate. There are three stages to the ritual: **Construction, Preparation and Execution.**

### Stage 1: Construction
Crafting a ritual to bond with a monster familiar is as much an art as it is a science.
*   **Steps:** 10 steps. Each step takes 5 consecutive full days of work.
*   **Checks:** After each week, make a check (Knowledge relevant to type, Sense Motive, or Research).
*   **DC:** 20 + candidate's CR (minimum 1).
*   **Success:** Must have at least 5 successful checks out of 10.

### Stage 2: Preparation
Gathering expensive and exotic materials.
*   **Base Purchase DC:** 15.
*   **Modifiers:** +1 per CR of candidate; -1 per successful Research check in Construction; +2 for exotic materials (max 5).
*   **Type Modifiers:** Dragon (+3), Immortal (+20), Kaijuu (+2), Kami (+2), Outsider (+2), Spirit (+1), Youkai (+2).
*   **Failure:** If the Wealth check fails, the ritual is ruined and must be started again.

### Stage 3: Execution
A simple level check modified by one ability score depending on the candidate type.
*   **DC:** 20 + candidate's Hit Dice (minimum 1).
*   **Ability Modifiers:**
    *   **Strength:** Animal, Giant, Monstrous Humanoid.
    *   **Dexterity:** Ooze, Vermin.
    *   **Constitution:** Elemental, Magical Beast.
    *   **Intelligence:** Construct, Fey, Humanoid.
    *   **Wisdom:** Plant, Undead.
    *   **Charisma:** Aberration, Dragon, Outsider.

| Factor | Bonus |
| :--- | :---: |
| Each successful Knowledge check (max 5) | +1 |
| Each successful Sense Motive check (max 5) | +1 |
| Two Wealth checks during Preparation | +5 |
| Exotic materials used in Preparation (max 5) | +1 (doubled if 2 Wealth checks made) |
| **Synergy Bonus (5+ ranks)** | |
| Diplomacy (Aberration, Dragon, Elemental, Outsider) | +2 |
| Handle Animal (Animal, Magical Beast, Vermin) | +2 |
| Intimidate (Giant, Fey, Humanoid, Monstrous Humanoid, Undead) | +2 |
| Repair (Constructs) | +2 |
| Survival (Ooze, Plant) | +2 |

## Bonded Familiars
Once the ritual is complete, the bonded familiar vanishes into the ether until summoned again. It follows the same rules as summons for death, injury, and duration.
*   **Summoning Rank:** 6 + half candidate's CR (max 14).
*   **Chakra Cost:** Chakra pool of familiar or twice its CR, whichever is higher.

### Familiar Advancement
If the familiar is capable of advancement, the character can perform an Advancement ritual.
*   **Construction:** 5 steps.
*   **Preparation:** Base DC 12.
*   **Limit:** Advance up to 5 hit dice at a time.
*   **Failure:** Familiar does not advance and cannot be summoned for 1d4 weeks. Failure by 10+ means it cannot be summoned for a month and requires a second check (failure on second check kills the familiar).
