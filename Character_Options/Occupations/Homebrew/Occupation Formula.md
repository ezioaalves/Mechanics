Step-by-Step Design Formula

Use these steps to create a balanced new occupation:

Step 1: Define the Wealth Tier

- **+1 or +2 Wealth:** Allows for multiple bonus skills, perhaps a bonus feat, and maybe a reputation bonus. (Examples: Clãs que tem que fazer muita coisa)

- **+3 Wealth:** Balances well with a few skills and maybe a reputation bonus, but rarely a feat. (Examples: Clã famoso, como o Hyuuga)

- **+4 Wealth:** Fewer other benefits, typically 2-3 skills maximum, very unlikely to have a feat. (Alto Clã de renome e cortesão, tipo os Yamanaka)

- **+6 Wealth:** Provides no extra skills, feats, or reputation bonuses beyond standard character creation. (Examples: Nobre ou qualquer coisa do tipo)


Step 2: Select Permanent Class Skills

2-3 skills that are thematic for the occupation. 
- _Guideline:_ Limit to a maximum of 3 unique skills.

Step 3: Assign Optional Benefits

Add a bonus feat or a +1 Reputation bonus if the occupation's wealth bonus is low enough to accommodate them (Tier 1 or 2).

- _Guideline:_ A bonus feat usually replaces the value of 1 or 2 other benefits. Occupations with bonus feats usually have a +1 or +2 Wealth bonus.

Step 4: Write Prerequisites

Define logical prerequisites based on the occupation's demands:

- **Age:** Usually a minimum age of 18-21.
- **Ability Scores:** Some demand a minimum score in a relevant ability (e.g., a _Doctor_ might require 13 Int or Wis; a _Blue Collar_ might require 13 Str or Con).

Occupation Design Formula

A new occupation should provide benefits roughly equivalent to existing ones, generally balancing the number of guaranteed class skills against the Wealth bonus provided.

|Benefit Category|Guidelines|
|---|---|
|**Prerequisite**|Optional. Usually simple (e.g., Age 18+, specific class skill already selected).|
|**Class Skills**|Select 2 or 3 skills that logically fit the occupation. These become permanent class skills for the character, regardless of their chosen base class(es).|
|**Bonus Feat**|Optional. If included, it usually replaces some other benefit, or is a specific, non-overpowered feat that fits the theme (e.g., a specific Weapon Proficiency, or an appropriate general feat).|
|**Reputation Bonus**|Optional +1 bonus. Often tied to high-profile or public-facing jobs (e.g., Celebrity, Law Enforcement).|
|**Wealth Bonus Increase**|The primary balancing factor. This is generally +1, +2, +3, +4, or +6.|
