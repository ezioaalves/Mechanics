# Races — Rules

*Source: NarutoD20 Redux v1.3, Chapter 2 – Races (p19–21). These are the new races presented for use, most of which are varieties of humans found around the world.*

See also: [[Human]], [[Gigantic]], [[Human_Earth]], [[Human_Fire]], [[Human_Lightning]], [[Human_Water]], [[Human_Wind]], [[Monstrous]], [[Smallfolk]], [[Trueblooded_Human]].

## Unusual Sizes

Some of the races in this chapter are not of Medium size, like humans are, which affects their equipment and statistics.

**Small:** Small characters gain a +1 size bonus to Defense and attack rolls, a +4 size bonus to Hide checks and suffer a −4 penalty to Grapple checks. Their carrying capacity is only ¾ of a Medium sized character, and their unarmed and natural attack damage dice are one size smaller (a d6 becomes a d4).

**Large:** Large characters gain the reverse (−1 penalty to Defense and attack, −4 to Hide and +4 to Grapple). Their carrying capacity is twice that of a Medium character, and their unarmed and natural attack damage dice are one size category larger (a d6 becomes a d8).

**Equipment:** Equipment for characters of a size different from Medium will have different statistics. Armor can be resized to fit a creature one size larger or smaller than its original intended wearer. Large size armors are 50% more expensive than Medium size ones. See Weapons (p65) and Armor (p69).

## Humans and Non-Humans

The basic D20 Modern rules are based on characters all being a 'normal' Human, but the races listed below are not. **When you create a non-Human, you select one less feat at first level, and reduce the basic skill point gain of every class by 1** (meaning you get 4 fewer skill points at level 1, and 1 fewer skill point at each level afterwards).

Racial entries use the following fields:

- **Size:** The size category of characters of this race. If not listed, the race is Medium size.
- **Speed:** The base land movement speed of the race. If nothing is listed, its speed is 30 ft.
- **Ability Modifiers:** Racial bonuses (or penalties) to your ability scores. They are applied after you have determined your ability scores.
- **Elemental Affinity:** If included, characters of that race automatically have the listed element as their primary affinity.
- **Languages:** All characters start with Read/Write Common and Speak Common, in addition to any listed.
- **Other Languages:** If present, these languages are commonly learned by members of this race.
- **Challenge Rating:** Increase the character's Challenge Rating by this amount (a statistic for your GM).
- **Level Adjustment (LA):** Some races carry a Level Adjustment. The character is treated as a number of levels higher for the purpose of how much XP it requires to level up and how much XP it grants when defeated. For all other purposes, these 'added levels' are not counted (you do not select class levels, do not increase your maximum skill rank, do not count the adjustment to determine feats or ability score modifiers gained by levels, and any level-based effects of spells, techniques, abilities, etc. do not count your level adjustment).

When creating characters at a level higher than 1, **reduce your starting level by your Level Adjustment.**

> **Example:** In a level 8 party, a Gigantic character (LA +2) would start at level 6, selecting only six class levels. The maximum number of skill points they could spend on any one skill would be 9, they would gain two feats from levels (levels 3 and 6), and would gain a single ability score increase (at level 4). The XP required for them to level up would be the same as the XP required by other characters to get to level 9.

## Age

As normal humans age, so do the newly presented races, though not always at the same rate. The ability adjustments are identical to those in the D20 Modern rulebook and are presented for convenience. The adjustments from Middle Age and beyond are **cumulative**.

**Maximum Age:** When a character ages enough to become venerable, the GM secretly rolls the Maximum Age dice for that race, and adds the result to the Venerable age. If the character reaches this maximum age, they will die of old age within one year (unless the maximum age increases before that time).

> **Example:** When a Gigantic character reaches the age of 60, the GM secretly rolls 2d10. If the result is 12, the character will die of old age before they turn 73 (but after they turn 72).
