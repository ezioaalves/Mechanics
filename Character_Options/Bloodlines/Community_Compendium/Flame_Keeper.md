# Flame Keeper

The Flame Keeper bloodline represents a deep connection to the ancient power of fire dragons. Those who possess this bloodline slowly take on the physical and spiritual aspects of dragons, eventually becoming a "Dragon Apotheosis".

## Requirements
To qualify to gain Flame Keeper bloodline levels, a character must fulfill all the following criteria.
*   **Skills**: Handle Animal 4 ranks.
*   **Feats**: Advanced Bloodline (Flame Keeper).
*   **Special**: Elemental Affinity (Fire), Con 14.

## Bloodline Abilities

### Moujuu Aishou
The character gains the Moujuu Aishou feat at first level for free. Except that the level adjustment is +2 instead of +1 (Attuned to Fire Dragons).

### Rage of the Dragon Blooded (Su)
The Keeper of the Fire dragon risks at all times of entering a deadly frenzy, attacking friends and foes without conscious. To combat the situation, the mix of the Dragon blood causes, the clan created a unique artifact that controls the rage as long as the Keeper wears it. Once the body gets used to the effects of the artifact, the Flame Keeper, any time the person stops wearing it they will enter the deadly frenzy. 

This applies a +6 Strength and Dexterity as he becomes a raging, mindless killing monster for the duration. He gains a +2 to Will save and each natural attack does a die larger of damage. The character cannot use any Charisma-, Dexterity-, or Intelligence-based skills (except Intimidate), the Concentration skill or any abilities that require patience or concentration, nor can he perform Chakra Control, Genjutsu or Ninjutsu techniques, except fire based Ninjutsu which the Keeper can auto-perform. This applies to any with a rank up to half his total hit die. 

The Keeper will eventually make a Fortitude save (DC 15 + the total number of rounds the Rage lasted) to negate the loss of consciousness from the rush of adrenaline after 1 minute. If the Fortitude save was successful, the Keeper will become Fatigued. There is no way other than to wear the Flame Keeper to prematurely end the rage. This ability can be used one per day. 

During frenzy, the keeper must attack those he perceives as foes to the best of his ability. Should he run out of enemies before his frenzy expires, his rampage continues. He must then attack the nearest creature (determine randomly if several potential foes are equidistant) and fight that opponent without regard to friendship or health (the target's or his own). A Will save can be made to resist attacking friendlies or striking killing blows with a DC equal to 15 + the total number of rounds the Rage has lasted. If the character is still under the effect of a rage ability, the fatigued condition does not apply until the rage ends—at which point the character is exhausted, not merely fatigued.

### Fang and Claw (Ex)
The character gains a bite and two claw attack forms. These attacks do damage as appropriate to the character’s size (1d6/1d4 for a medium sized character). Though bite attacks gain the bonus of the character's full strength, claw attacks only gain 1/2 of the keeper's strength modifier rounded down when applied to damage. 

Most strangers will not be comfortable around a person who has noticeable fangs and claws. Dragons strike fear wherever they roam, and lizardfolk do not have nicest reputation. The more closeted a community is from exotic-looking people, the more likely it is that the character will receive a negative penalty (-1 to -5) on charisma based skill rolls. The character can avoid the negative penalty if they take care to conceal their lizardlike attributes through a Disguise check (DC 10). 

**Fang and Claw II, III, and IV**: Each allows the damage for the Keeper's claw and bite natural weapons to increases by one step, as if the creature’s size had increased by one category: 1d4, 1d6, 1d8, 2d6, 3d6. Disguise checks to hide the Keeper's lizard like appearance now increase by an additional 2, per form (This disregards the first form of Fang and Claw, as it sets the base 10 DC).

### Scales (Ex)
The keeper's body sprouts scales that are fiery red. These scales cover the belly and back but begin to thin out heading toward the legs and arms. There may be some scales around the neck but very rarely do they appear on the face. The scales provide a natural armor bonus of +2 and Fire resistance of +10. 

**Scales II, III, and IV**: Each allows the user +1 additional natural armor bonus and +5 Fire resistance. Rank IV counts the keeper as immune to fire. Disguise checks to hide the Keeper's lizard like appearance now increase by an additional 2, per form.

### Dragon Wings (Ex)
The blood of the dragon runs strong in the character. He is able to grow wings that are smaller versions of dragon wings, allowing the character to fly at their land speed with average maneuverability. A medium or heavy load that affects land speed affects flying speed proportionately. 

**Dragon Wings II**: Allows the keeper's wings to adapt better to its body, thus doubling flying speed (maximum 120 feet). This also allows the Keeper to make 'Wing Attacks' that deal 1d4 damage (as a Medium-sized character). These attacks gain only 1/2 of the Keeper's strength modifier (rounded down) when applied to damage. Disguise checks to hide the Keeper's lizard like appearance now increase by an additional 4, per form.

### Dragon Apotheosis (Ex)
A Keeper is recognized fully among the dragonkin he is entrusted with, thus gaining rank and granted power among them. He gains +6 to Strength and Dexterity, and a +2 to Constitution. His eyes are honed, as that of a dragon, and thus he gains Darkvision 60 feet and lowlight vision. Additionally, he now loses Rage of the Dragon Blooded. Disguise checks to hide the Keeper's lizard like appearance now increase by an additional 8.

## Fire Dragon Companion

### Fire Dragon
The small scales of a wyrmling red dragon are a bright glossy scarlet, making the dragon easily spotted.
*   **Type**: Small dragon
*   **Hit Dice**: 1d12+2 (8 hp)
*   **Mass Threshold**: 50
*   **Initiative**: +0
*   **Speed**: 40 ft, Fly 150 ft. (poor)
*   **Defense**: 17 (+0 Dex, +1 Size, +6 Natural), Touch 11, Flat-Footed 17
*   **Base Attack/Grapple**: +1 / -4
*   **Attack**: +3 melee (1d6+2, bite)
*   **Full Attack**: +3 melee (1d6, bite), -2 melee (1d4+2, 2 claws)
*   **Fighting Space/Reach**: 5 ft by 5 ft / 5 ft
*   **Allegiance**: Companion
*   **Saves**: Fort +2, Ref +2, Will +2
*   **Action Points**: 0; **Chakra Points**: 2
*   **Reputation**: +0; **Wealth**: +0; **Learn**: +0
*   **Abilities**: Str 15, Dex 10, Con 15, Int 10, Wis 11, Cha 10.
*   **Skills**: Hide +4, Listen +4, Move Silently +4, Search +4, Spot +4, Taijutsu +4
*   **Feats**: Alertness
*   **Special Abilities**: Immune to Sleep, Hold and Paralysis effects, Immunity to Fire.

### Breath Weapon (Su)
A fire dragon has one type of breath weapon, a cone of fire. It does 1d8 fire damage and an additional 1d8 every 3 HD. A fire dragon may use this once every 1d4 rounds. Using the breath weapon is an attack action. If the weapon deals damage, creatures caught in the area can attempt Reflex saves to half the damage, the save DC is 10 + 1/2 dragon’s HD + dragon’s Con modifier.

### Skills
The owner may pick 3 skills that are considered class skills for their fire dragon. All dragons have skill points equal to (6 + Int modifier, minimum 1) × (Hit Dice + 3). Most dragons purchase the following skills at the maximum ranks possible: Listen, Search, and Spot. The remaining skill points are generally spent on Concentration, Diplomacy, Escape Artist, Intimidate, Knowledge (any), Sense Motive, at a cost of 1 skill point per rank. All these skills are considered class skills for dragons.

### Water and Ice Vulnerability (Ex)
A fire dragon takes 50% more damage when subjected to Water or Ice techniques or abilities that deal Water or Ice damage.

### Speak Language
A fire dragon may speak one language that its owner knows.

### Advancement
A fire dragon advances based on the chart provided in the Core Book (see Moujuu Aishou) except for as noted below:
*   **Base Attack Bonus**: A fire dragon has a good base attack bonus.
*   **Saving Throws**: A fire dragon has good saves for Fortitude, Reflex and Will.
*   **Skill Points**: A fire dragon gains 6 skill points per hit die, and an additional feat once every 4 HD. It must meet the prerequisites normally.
