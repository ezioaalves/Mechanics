### BONE WEAPON (EX)

As a move-equivalent action that does not provoke attacks of opportunity, can extract a Bone Weapon of
certain sizes from one of their limbs. If the weapon is Diminutive or Tiny, they are usable and do not render a limb disabled. If the user creates a Small, Medium or Large Bone Weapon, the limb becomes unusable for 10 rounds - 1 per level (minimum 1). An unusable arm cannot be used to make skill checks, hand seals or wield weapons, and incurs a -2 penalty to Strength and Grapple checks. An unusable leg halves your movement speed and makes you unable to run, incurring a -1 AC penalty and a -4 penalty to opposed Bull Rush, Trip or Overrun checks. The user may create an unlimited number of Diminutive and Tiny Bone Weapons at no cost, while creating larger Bone Weapons costs 1, 2, or 3 Chakra respectively for Small, Medium or Large. Weapons may deal bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing at the user’s choice, this is made upon creating the weapon. A GM may over rule a weapon that makes no sense such as a Diminutive weapon dealing bludgeoning (bone needles should obviously only deal piercing). A Bone Weapon lasts for 6 hours before turning to dust. The user is considered proficient with all their Bone Weapons. If you have Exotic Melee Weapon Proficiency, you can wield a Large Bone Weapon in one hand. Characters without this bloodline cannot become proficient in this weapon without the feat Exotic Melee Weapon Proficiency (Bone Weapons). If the user is a different size than Medium, adjust the weapon's sizes, damage and hit points appropriately.

### BONE ARMOR (EX)

As an instant action, you can spend up to 5 Chakra to gain DR X/Dark Iron for a number of rounds equal to 1 + their Con modifier, where X is the amount of Chakra spent.

### EXTENDED LIFE (EX)

You add a number of years to all of your maximum age and to the limits of all age categories for your race,
though you cannot be set back from the age category you are currently in.

### SHIKOTSUMYAKU (EX)

Your Bone Weapons gain an enhancement bonus to attack and damage rolls. While your Bone Armor DR gains a bonus equal to the level of Shikotsumyaku, this bonus adds to the DR as well as the rounds the ability lasts (i.e. a +5 bonus would mean a user can spend 1 chakra and gain +6 DR/Dark Iron for 6 rounds).

### DEATHLESS (EX)

You gain a bonus to Fortitude saves against massive damage, and become capable of regenerating limbs you have lost. Tiny body parts like fingers and toes take 1d4+2 days to regrow, large parts like hands and feet take 3d4+6 days to regrow, and large limbs like an arm or leg takes 4d6 weeks. You are fatigued while the regeneration takes place, and cannot relieve or ignore the fatigue by any means. In addition, you recover from Strength, Dexterity and Constitution damage twice as fast while resting.

### HARI KINKOTSU (BONE NEEDLES)

Taijutsu (Strike; Requires Bone Weapon (a)) [Strike, Kaguya Clan Hijutsu] Rank: 2 (C); Learn DC: 15, 3 successes; Perform: 2 (DC 15); Time: 1 attack action; Components: E, Mas; Range: Close; Target: Target(s); Duration: Instant; Save: None; Cost: 1 per bone needle. By forming a piece of thin very sharp bone in the user’s mouth they are able to then fire the needle shaped bone fragment utilizing some Chakra. Every bone needle deals the base damage for a standard needle, and benefits from anything that would increase using needles or bone weapons. Each needle can be fired as an attack action at your highest attack bonus (or multiple based upon your BAB). Empower – Spend 1 Chakra to fire an additional needle, up to 1 per level (max 5). Mastery – Every odd step increases the damage done by the needle by +1 (+4 max).

### ISHIZUKURIJIN SHIKKAN (STONEMAN DISEASE)

Taijutsu (Strike; Requires Bone Weapon (a) Togeyomi no Jutsu) [Kaguya Clan Hijutsu] Rank: 10 (A); Learn DC: 25, 5 successes; Perform: 13 (DC 27); Time: 1 attack action; Components: Mas, P; Range: 10 feet; Target: 1 Target; Duration: Instant; Save: Fortitude negates; Cost: 3. This deadly technique infuses the victim with a bit of the user's own DNA which forces the victim's own bones to reproduce uncontrollably. This often proves to be fatal for those of poor Constitution. The user strikes the target, if the attack deals damage the target immediately makes a Fortitude save to resist the disease. If the target fails their saving throw they take 1d6 Con damage. After 1 minute the target must make another saving throw at the same DC, failure incurs another 2d6 Con damage. This disease continues to ravage the body of the target once each day for another 1d6 Con damage until the victim successfully
makes a saving throw. Each time the victim fails bone spurs jut out of their skin abruptly and painfully. This disease can be removed by a properly trained Medical Ninja of at least 5th level or higher by making a Treat Injury (DC 25) check. Mastery – Every odd step adds an additional +1 to the Con damage each time it is incurred. At the 5th step of mastery, the DC for the saving throw increases by +1 and the Treat Injury check increases by 5.

### KAMARATSU NO MAI (DANCE OF THE LARCH)

Taijutsu (Enhancement) (Requires Bone Weapon (a)) [Kaguya Clan Hijutsu] Rank: 8 (A); Learn DC: 23, 5 successes; Perform: 11 (DC 25); Time: 1 full-round action; Components: Mas, P; Range: Personal; Target: You; Duration: 1 round/level (D); Saving Throws: None; Cost: 10. Using this technique, the user uses her bloodline to extract her bones to a greater degree than with any of the previous dances. Out of everywhere on her body, forearm, hands, ribs, back, shoulders, and even her legs, the bones stick out in a protective and lethal armor. She gains a +3 natural armor bonus to defense, and all attacks made against her with a hand-held weapon of Small or smaller size or unarmed attacks deal 1d4 points of piercing damage to the attacker in return. In addition, once every 3 rounds, she may give up all her attacks for the round in order to spin wildly and make an attack at her highest attack bonus against all adjacent opponents, as per the Whirlwind Attack feat. All unarmed attacks made in this state deal damage as a medium Bone Weapon. Mastery – The second and fourth steps of mastery increase the user’s Natural Armor bonus by +1. The first and third steps of mastery reduce the time between Whirlwind Attacks by 1 round.

This power functions whether the user has a free hand to grapple or not. This attack may also be
used on any physical bonds that give the user the entangled, grappled, or immobilized conditions. The user may pay the cost of the technique again to extend the duration of the technique when the technique ends. Empower – Spend 1 Chakra to produce additional spikes, each adds an additional 1d6, up to 1 per 2 levels (max 4d6). Mastery – Every odd step increases the damage for each set of spikes by +1 (+3 max).

### KOTSU BUNSHIN NO JUTSU (BONE CLONE TECHNIQUE)

Ninjutsu (Requires; Bone Armor (a)) [Kaguya Clan Kinjutsu] Rank: 6 (B); Learn DC: 20, 5 successes; Perform: 8 (DC 21); Time: 1 full-round action; Components: C, S, E; Range: 50 feet (see text); Effect: Duplicate(s) of the user; Duration: 1 minute/level (D); Saving Throws: None; Cost: 3 (base; see text). This technique allows the user to remove some of her bones and grow them into copies of herself. Creating a Kotsu Bunshin not only costs Chakra, but also divides the user's physical energy, which is why this technique is forbidden in the first place. For this reason, the user suffers 1d4 points of damage per clone created. The user cannot control any other type of clone (including simple Bunshin) while using this technique. A Kotsu Bunshin can be created in any square it could normally occupy within the technique’s range, but may not stray further than 1 mile from the user, and may be created in any location that can hold it within 50 feet. Once a clone reaches 0 hit points, strays too far from the user, or the duration expires, it disappears in a puff of smoke. The clones count as though their Chakra Pool was half of the user’s current Chakra Pool for purposes of Sense Chakra. While the user can replace destroyed clones by performing this technique multiple times, she cannot control more than 1 Kotsu Bunshin per level; any attempts to create additional clones beyond this limit automatically fail. The user can dismiss any number of clones of her choice instead of simply dismissing them all.
Although a Kotsu Bunshin is not capable of independent, sentient thought, it does share a one way mental bond with the user, and thus obeys the mental commands of the original to the letter. A Kotsu Bunshin is its own person and if caught in a Genjutsu, only the replica will be affected; not the user and/or the whole group of clones. Anything experienced by the clone will be remembered by the user when the technique expires, but does not confer any extra experience or other similar benefits upon the user. The clone is genuine flesh-and-blood and carries the same equipment as the user, except chakra dependent items, poisons, and other chemicals. Any mastercraft bonuses are not carried over to the clone's equipment. Any items carried by the Kotsu Bunshin have only half the hit points of the original and 0 hardness, and disappear along with the clone when it is dismissed or destroyed. A Kotsu Bunshin has 1 hit point per 2 levels of the user, and shares the user’s Defense at a -4 penalty, the user’s saving throws at a -2 penalty, and the user’s attack bonus at a -3 penalty, although no clone can attack more than three times per round. The clone also shares all the user’s skills, feats, talents, and special abilities. However, the clone does not gain any of the user’s templates, and they do not gain any bloodline abilities beyond Bone Weapon and Bone Armor. A clone does not benefit from any temporary bonuses the user may currently have (such as Speed Ranks or Tadayou). A Kotsu Bunshin can perform any technique known by the user of Rank 4 or lower, using the same skill modifier and skill threshold as the user. However, the clone cannot spend its own chakra on the technique and must borrow from the user’s chakra pool to pay the cost. Any drawbacks from a technique performed by a clone affect both the clone performing it and the user. If a clone uses a technique to create additional Kotsu Bunshin, the additional clones are controlled by the user and count against the user’s limit on the number of clones she can control. Although a clone can use techniques, it cannot perform any activities requiring particular attention, such as Sense Chakra, detonating an Exploding Tag, or carrying a serious conversation. Empower – The user can create additional clones for 3 additional chakra per clone (max 1/level). Mastery – Each odd step reduces a clone’s penalty to Defense by 1. At the 5th step the a clone may use up to a rank 6 technique.

### TESSENKA NO MAI (DANCE OF THE CLEMATIS)

Ninjutsu (Requires Bone Weapon (a) and Deathless (a)) [Kaguya Clan Kinjutsu]
Rank: 9 (S); Learn DC: 25, 7 successes; Perform: 13 (DC 29); Time: 1 attack action; Components: C, P, M; Range: Personal; Target: You; Duration: Instant or 1 round/level; Saving Throws: None; CR: No; Cost: 14. This extremely dangerous technique is considered the fourth dance of the Kaguya style. It was forbidden for an excellent reason by the fourth leader, Kaguya Chinmei, because in involves concentrating all of one's strength in one place or pulling out one's spine. Only the most talented and gifted in the arts of the bloodline were ever able to master the technique, anyway; the rest died in attempting to learn it. When performing this technique, the user choses one of two modes to use the technique

### for:

Vine: By pulling out his spine, the user is able to form a bone weapon of one size category larger than he, and that can be used both as a reach weapon that can expand to allow him to reach enemies 15 feet away as well as adjacent foes. The weapon can be used like both a spiked chain, whip, and a bone weapon, therefore bonus feats and abilities that apply to either of the weapons apply to the Vine. The weapon deals triple damage on a critical hit and will vanish normally (see Shikotsumyaku for details). Immediately upon using the technique in this manner, the user must make a Fortitude save (DC 15) or become paralyzed for 1 minute and fatigued for 1 hour afterwards. After using this technique, the character cannot pull out his spine in the same manner for 24 hours. Flower: By concentrating all of his power into either one of his arm, he is able to extract the thickest and most dense bone in his body. The weapon extracted counts as a Colossal bone weapon that threatens a critical hit on a natural roll of 18, 19 or 20. If the user was of Large size when using this technique, it deals 5d6 points of piercing damage instead of 4d6. The "flower" lasts for 1 round per level and, once it fades, will leave the arm it was used on crushed and unusable for 1 hour (see Shikotsumyaku for details). Mastery – Each even step of mastery adds an additional 5 ft. when using Vine. Each odd step of mastery lessens the Fortitude save by 1. The fifth step mastery treats the user as Large size when using Flower. The fifth step of mastery the user is no longer treated as fatigued when failing the Fortitude save.
