# Ketsueki

This ability appears in certain children, regardless of clan. The reason this appears is still unknown, it could be a genetic abnormality or an actual, albeit spread out, bloodline.

## Requirements
To qualify to gain Ketsueki bloodline levels, a character must fulfill all the following criteria.
*   **Ability Scores**: Constitution 15
*   **Feats**: Advanced Bloodline (Ketsueki), Blood Mage (if taken for the Bloodline, Fuinjutsu requirement is only 4 ranks).

## Bloodline Abilities

### Advanced Blood (Su)
The character can pay up to half the chakra cost of a jutsu or 5 (whichever is higher) when using their Blood Mage feat.

### Blood Weapon (Su)
The character may use their own blood to create any weapon that they are proficient with of the given size category or lower as a move action. They take 1 Constitution damage per size category (1 for tiny, 2 for small, etc.) and lose health and chakra accordingly. When they return the blood to their body, a swift action, they regain the lost Constitution, health and chakra.

If disarmed of the weapon, the user must make a concentration check (DC equal to the attack roll that was made to disarm them), if they succeed, the weapon breaks into a mist and returns to the user's blood stream, he may then reform it as normal, if he fails the concentration check, the weapon turns to normal blood and may not be reabsorbed and the weapon's creator must heal the Constitution damage normally (1 point per day of rest, as temporary damage).

A Blood Weapon may be sundered as a normal weapon, having hardness equal to twice the user's Con modifier and Hit Points equal to twice his Con score. If the weapon is sundered, it turns to normal blood and may not be reabsorbed and the weapon's creator must heal the Constitution damage normally.

A character may only have one Blood Weapon created at a time (see Blood Weapon (Utility)) and the Constitution damage may not be healed as long as the weapon is formed. A Blood Weapon counts as chakra enhanced for the purposes of overcoming damage reduction. A character may have Weapon enhancement seals placed on their forearm (using up body seal slots accordingly) to enhance their Blood Weapon.

### Blood Training (Su)
The character may gain a bonus to learn certain techniques equal to half their character level. This bonus applies to any technique requiring the Blood Mage feat as a prerequisite.

### Gift of the Blood (Su)
The character may take a full round action to spawn two Blood Weapons at once. These Blood weapons are formed in each hand and have a maximum size of one size category less than the character's normal maximum Blood Weapon size. A character must place one enhancement seal on each forearm to affect both weapons (taking up two slots). A character takes Two-Weapon Fighting penalties for wielding two Blood Weapons at once. In addition, whenever the character forms a Blood Weapon into a weapon that can be thrown (such as a shuriken or a kunai) they may make thrown attacks as normal. After the weapon is thrown, the blood disperses and reforms into the user's body at the end of their current turn.

### Blood Weapon (Utility) (Su)
The character may ignore the blood component for any summoning jutsu that deals no Constitution damage from the loss of blood. Alternatively, when using a summoning jutsu with a blood component the character may take an amount of temporary Constitution damage to give the summoned creature 1d6 temporary hit points for every point of Constitution damage the summoner takes.

### Infectious Blood (Su)
The character may apply a dose of their blood to any non-bludgeoning weapon (an attack action). This dose lasts until a creature is struck with the weapon. The first time this weapon strikes a creature while covered, the character's blood seeps into the opponent's veins, constricting them. That opponent takes a -5 penalty to the next save he makes against the blood user. This effect lasts for 24 hours. When applying Infectious Blood to a Blood Weapon created by the user, they may use Infectious Blood as a swift action. This ability may be used once per day for every 4 levels the user has.

### Living Blood (Su)
The character may make a melee touch attack against any creature infected with its Infected Blood ability and may spend any amount of chakra up to their character level. The opponent takes 1d6 damage per point of chakra sacrificed (half with a Fortitude save, DC 10 + 1/2 character level of the blood user + user's Con modifier) as the user's blood bursts from their veins. This ends Infectious Blood.
