HEBIHA
Few shinobi have ever been seen in possession of this
strange and rare bloodline. Over the course of the
character’s life, he will gradually gain traits related to
those of a snake, namely the formation of retractable
fangs and a poison gland that resides in the character’s
mouth. Albeit a freakish bloodline, there is no doubt that
the Hebiha blood is powerful.

### NATURAL WEAPON (BITE) (EX)

The creature gains a bite attack at 1st level. This is in the form of retractable fangs that normally lie dormant, but can be extended as a free action on the creature’s turn that does not provoke an attack of opportunity. The bite deals slashing, bludgeoning or piercing damage appropriate to the creature’s size category, which is 1d4 for a Medium creature.

### SNAKE SENSE (SU)

The creature can activate this as a move action that does not provoke an attack of opportunity. The creature gains the Temorsense extraordinary ability with a range of 30 feet. In addition, the creature gains a +2 bonus to listen checks while this ability is active. This ability costs 1 point of chakra per minute to maintain.

### VENOM STORES (EX)

At 5th level the creature develops glands in the roof of the mouth that can store a single dose of poison. Doing so is a full-round action that may provoke an attack of opportunity, and costs 3 points of chakra. The poison must be an injury-type poison, and on a successful attack that deals at least one point of damage, the target is exposed to the poison and suffers its full effects. The glands can hold only 1 dose of poison initially. This ability increases to 2 doses of poison at 12th level, and 3 doses of poison at 20th level. At the GM’s discretion, this may have to be the same-type of poison.

### SERPENT CRAWL (EX)

At 9th level, the creature can opt to move on all fours up to half his maximum land speed. Doing so is a move action that provokes an attack of opportunity. Whilst on all fours, he suffers the penalties for being prone as normal, except that he takes no penalty for attacking with his bite. At 17th level, the creature can move as freely as though he was on two legs, and no longer suffers any of the penalties normally associated with being prone.
