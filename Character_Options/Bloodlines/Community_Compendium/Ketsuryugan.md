KETSURYUGAN [DOUJUTSU]
The Ketsuryugan is a doujutsu kekkei genkai, which
appears in certain individuals of the Chinoike clan. The
Ketsuryugan is recognized by its reddish color, giving a
special appearance for the users of this doujutsu,
characterized as holders of "eyes as red as blood". The
Ketsuryugan confers genjutsu casting abilities. Its power
earned it comparison to the Three Great Doujutsu.
The Ketsuryugan also gave the users the ability to
manipulate the user's blood, or more accurately the iron
in the blood, allowing the user to not only manipulate
their blood, but any liquid with high iron concentration.

### KETSURYUGAN (SU)

The character activates this ability as an attack action that does not provoke an attack of opportunity. He gains an insight bonus to Defense, Initiative and Spot checks and Genjutsu checks to detect illusions, as well as checks made to read lips or memorize a string of numbers, words or certain details (such as a crime scene). The character can concentrate for a move-equivalent action to gain the ability to See Chakra for as long as he concentrates, plus 5 rounds. Concentrating this way is the same as if he were concentrating on a technique. The Ketsuryugan costs 1 point of chakra every other round to maintain. If the character is blinded, he gains none of the benefits of the Ketsuryugan. Unless caught flat-footed, immobilized or feinted against, the character cannot lose his Dexterity bonus to defense while the Ketsuryugan is active. Deactivating this ability is a free action.

### BLOOD COMPANION (EX)

The character may turn a body of water into a Blood Companion as a standard action that does not provoke an attack of opportunity. In order to create a Blood Companion, the character needs a small source of water (16 liters or more) nearby and must sacrifice 5 hit points as they cut themselves and bleed into the body of water as a free action. A character may create a Blood Companion of a size equal to the size listed in the table above, but may elect to create a smaller than the max category. Each Blob size requires x4 more liters of water than the previous size (64 for Large, 256 for Huge and so on), as well as an additional 5 hit points sacrificed per larger size. A Blood Companion may act on the round after it is created, and acts on its creator's initiative. The Blood Companion creature is listed below. A Blood Companion has HD equal to its creator's HD. A character may not create more than the character’s Wisdom modifier of Blood Companions at a time. When a character creates a Blood Companion past their limit then the oldest active Blood Companion is destroyed.

A Blood Companion takes 50% more Doton damage when subjected to Doton techniques or abilities that deal Doton damage.

### CREATOR LINK (SU)

A Blood Companion is linked to its creator mentally at all times. As long as a Blood Companion is within 100 feet of its creator its creator may send it commands and orders as if it were a Clone. This communication is solely one-way, and the Blood Companion cannot send messages back. The Blood Companion understands commands from its creator perfectly, but does not speak any languages and can only communicate in emotions and very simple concepts. Blood Companions are always loyal and obedient to their creator, and cannot be forced to betray their creator no matter the circumstances.

### IMPROVED GRAB (EX)

If the creature hits with a melee weapon (usually a claw or bite attack), it deals normal damage and attempts to start a grapple as a free action, doing so without provoking attacks of opportunity. No initial touch attack is required. Unless otherwise stated, improved grab works only against opponents at least one size category smaller than the creature. A Small or smaller creature using improved grab does not apply its grapple modifier to its grapple check. The creature has the option to conduct the grapple normally, or simply use the part of its body it used in the improved grab to hold the opponent. If it chooses to do the latter, it takes a –20 penalty on grapple checks but is not considered grappled itself; the creature does not lose its Dexterity bonus to Defense, still threatens an area, and can use its remaining attacks against other opponents. A successful hold does not deal additional damage unless the creature also has the constrict ability (see above). If the creature does not constrict, each successful grapple check it makes during successive rounds automatically deals the damage given for the attack that established the hold. When a creature gets a hold after an improved grab attack, it pulls the opponent into its space. This act does not provoke attacks of opportunity. The creature is not considered grappled while it holds the opponent, so it still threatens adjacent squares and retains its Dexterity bonus. It can even move, provided it can drag the opponent’s weight.
