KYOME [DOUJUTSU]
Known as “Devil Eye”, this little-known bloodline
from the Ryu clan from the Village Hidden in the Grove
aids the user in performing genjutsu by allowing them to
see into their opponent’s mind and see their fears. Some
of the most powerful in this clan even unlock greater
proficiency with it, manifesting the fear without so
much as a thought on their part.
When not activated the eyes of the user are pure black,
save for the iris which is white. Upon activating the
Kyome the eye changes, the sclera (white part of the eye)
and the iris turn completely black, while the pupil turns
white. While gazing into these eyes you feel them
searching the deepest part of your mind, finding the very
thing you fear the most.

### KYOME (SU)

The character activates this ability as an attack action that does not provoke an attack of opportunity. While Kyome is active the character gains an insight bonus to all Genjutsu checks and checks to dispel Genjutsu. The character can concentrate for a move-equivalent action to gain the ability to See Chakra for as long as he concentrates, plus 5 rounds. Concentrating this way is the same as if he was concentrating on a technique. Kyome costs 1 point of chakra per round to maintain. If the character is blinded, he/she gains none of the benefits of Kyome. Deactivating this ability is a free action.

### KYOFU NO KOKEI (SU)

This ability is also known as “Fear Sight”, it is the signature ability of the Ryu clan. Once per encounter as
an attack action that provokes an attack of opportunity while Kyome is active the user may probe his/her enemy’s mind for their fears. The target, if aware, may attempt to avert their eyes as per the standard rules. Should the target fail to avert their eyes, or is unaware of this attempt and meets the user’s gaze must pass a will save (DC: 10+ (Kyome bonus*1.5)+ the user’s Cha Mod), if successful the target pushes the user out of their mind, and is now aware that something is wrong if they weren’t before. Should they fail the user finds a “minor” fear of the subject, for the next three rounds the target suffers a -1 to their will save against Genjutsu cast by the user of this technique if it involves their minor fear (the GM decides, and lets the player know what this minor fear is). At 7th level, the user’s skill grows and may now find an “average” fear. The target suffers a -2 instead of the 1 penalty against Genjutsu involving their “average” fear. At 11th level, the user may spend 8 chakra while discerning an “average” fear the user may immediately create an illusion of the target’s “average” fear, forcing him/her to make a will save with the -4 penalty against a DC of 10+ Kyome bonus*1.5 + Cha Mod or be shaken for up to 1 round per level. The target may make another save every round to end the cowering early. At 15th level the user may now find a “greater” fear. The target suffers a -3 penalty instead of -2 or -1 against Genjutsu involving their “greater” fear. At 20th level this ability has fully developed, by spending 15 chakra when discerning one “greater” fear the user may immediately create an illusion of the target’s “greater” fear, forcing him/her to make a will save with the -6 penalty against a DC of 10+ Kyome bonus*1.5 + the user’s Cha Mod or fall prone trembling in fear and unable to take any actions for 1d4+1 rounds. (Note: the target is not helpless).

### GENJUTSU RESISTANCE (EX)

Due to their natural skill in Genjutsu, the members of the Ryu clan start to develop a natural resistance to them. The character gains the specified bonus as an enhancement bonus to resist any and all Genjutsu; this ability is only active while their eye is activated.
