# Knowledge (Dungeoneering)

**Ability:** Intelligence (Int)
**Trained Only:** No (untrained checks limited to DC 10)
**Armor Check Penalty:** No

Knowledge (Dungeoneering) covers caverns, ruins, the deep underground, hazards of subterranean travel, and the creatures (mostly aberrations) that live in those places. Per the [Skill Conversion Table](../../House_Rules/Skill%20-%20Conversion.md), it absorbs the d20 Modern / L5R-flavoured skill **Knowledge (Shadowlands)** — the corruption-tainted, Taint-warped underworld that in a Rokugan-flavoured setting like Kaihou maps directly to the **Forward Bastion's lower tunnels**, the **collapsed Foundry sublevels**, and the **chakra-rusted caverns** beneath Kanigakure.

In Kaihou this is the **spelunker / underground-route specialist / aberration-scholar** skill — knowing what lives down there, what the air will do to you over a week, which collapsed shaft connects to which active tunnel, and how to recognise a corruption-tainted creature before it bites.

*Naruto D20 has no entry for this skill in the "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section — base rules apply.*

---

## Description

A character with Knowledge (Dungeoneering) recalls the layout of the **Forward Bastion** disaster zone, the routes through the **Foundry's lower mechanical guts**, the deep-shaft hazards a Kumanoi sliding-trap specialist exploits, and the natural-history of aberrant cave fauna. They recognise a chakra-corrupted creature on sight, know which subterranean fungi are edible versus those that *eat back*, and read cavern geology for collapse-risk and water table.

The skill of any character who **specialises in operating below the surface** — Sendo Hunter tunnel-pursuit specialists, Hoshigake smuggler-route scouts, Dattoumaru ambush specialists who exploit corridor geometry, Yoimura sapper-engineers who plan Wall-undercut defences.

## Check

### General DCs *(Pathfinder)*

| Question | DC |
|---|---|
| Identify a common cavern hazard or recognise a familiar subterranean feature | 10 |
| Navigate a known cavern network from memory | 15 |
| Recall an obscure subterranean ecosystem or read collapse-risk geology | 20 |
| Recall esoteric deep-underground geography or aberrant ecology | 25+ |

### Identify an Aberration / Tainted Creature *(Pathfinder)*

- **DC = 10 + creature's CR** for Aberrations and (in Kaihou) chakra-corrupted creatures.
- Each 5 over the DC reveals an additional useful detail.

### Subterranean Navigation *(Pathfinder)*

- Provides a **+2 synergy bonus** to [Survival](Survival.md) checks made underground at **2+ ranks** (the underground analogue to Survival's surface-navigation bonus).
- Provides a **+2 synergy bonus** to [Perception](Perception.md) checks made underground at **2+ ranks** (recognising hazards at a distance — bad-air zones, hidden shafts, the chitter of a tunnel-dwelling aberration).

### Corruption Recognition *(absorbed from d20 Modern / L5R-style Knowledge (Shadowlands))*

| Question | DC |
|---|---|
| Recognise a Taint / chakra-corruption stigma on a creature or object | 10 |
| Identify the specific source of a corruption | 15 |
| Recall the corruption's progression and effects on humanoids | 20 |
| Recall a remedy, suppression rite, or cleansing method | 25+ |

> *In Kaihou, the "Taint" maps to **rust-chakra corruption** — the residue of pre-village industrial chakra-refining that pools in the Iron Shell's lower caverns and warps anything that lives in it for long.*

## Action

Usually **no action** (passive recall). Researching cavern maps or aberrant taxonomies combines with [Linguistics](Linguistics.md) and takes 1d4 hours.

---

## Try Again

**No.** The check represents what you know.

---

## Special

- **Untrained** Knowledge (Dungeoneering) checks are capped at DC 10.
- You can **take 10**. You **cannot take 20**.
- With library / cartographer-archive access, the cap is lifted; each check takes 1d4 hours.
- The **Educated** feat (d20 Modern) grants **+2** on any two Knowledge subcategories.

---

## Synergy

*(Naruto D20 / d20 Modern synergy thresholds converted via the −3 rank-prereq rule. Original "5 ranks" → **2 ranks**.)*

- A character with **2 or more ranks** in Knowledge (Dungeoneering) gains a **+2 bonus** on **[Survival](Survival.md)** checks made underground. *(Pathfinder.)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in Knowledge (Dungeoneering) gains a **+2 bonus** on **[Perception](Perception.md)** checks made underground. *(Kaihou — the underground-hazard sense.)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in **[Knowledge (Nature)](Knowledge%20(Nature).md)** gains a **+2 bonus** on Knowledge (Dungeoneering) checks involving subterranean ecology (fungi, blind cavern fauna, lichen-based food webs). *(Naruto D20 — natural-science theory feeds aberrant ecology.)*

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Naruto D20:** No "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" entry. Synergies preserved with the −3 rank conversion.
- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/knowledge](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/knowledge/)):** Backbone — caverns, dungeons, aberrations, oozes scope; the standard DC framework; identify-aberration mechanic (DC 10 + CR); the Survival / Perception synergies for underground operation.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 76 — Knowledge subcategories) / L5R-style Knowledge (Shadowlands):** Absorbed — corruption-recognition, Taint progression, source-identification. Kaihou flavour: **rust-chakra corruption** in place of the L5R Shadowlands Taint.

**Conflict resolution:** Knowledge (Shadowlands) is the L5R-extension d20 Modern subcategory the conversion table explicitly routes here. It folds cleanly because both the PF Pathfinder scope and the L5R scope share an "underground / aberrant / corrupted" thematic centre. The Kaihou re-flavour (rust-chakra corruption ≈ Taint) is the only setting-specific touch.
