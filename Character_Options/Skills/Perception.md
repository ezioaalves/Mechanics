# Perception

**Ability:** Wisdom (Wis)
**Trained Only:** No
**Armor Check Penalty:** No

Perception is the catch-all sensory skill in Pathfinder, covering everything a character might notice with sight, sound, smell, taste, or touch. In Kaihou it replaces the four separate d20 Modern skills **Spot, Listen, Search,** and **Investigate**, plus the Naruto D20 carry-overs of those skills.

---

## Description

Perception is the skill that determines whether a character notices something — a hidden creature, a whispered word, a footprint in the soil, a faint scent of smoke, a clue concealed at a crime scene. A character makes Perception checks reactively (the GM calls for them) or actively (when the character spends a move action to look or listen).

In Kaihou's converted system, Perception is the default skill for *every* sensory question. Where an older d20 Modern rule would call for Spot, Listen, Search, or Investigate, call for Perception instead.

### Investigate a Scene *(adapted from d20 Modern Investigate)*

A character can use Perception to apply forensic-style analysis to a clue they have already found. Perception does not *create* clues that don't exist; it extracts additional information from what is already there.

- **Base DC** to analyse a clue: **15**

| Circumstance | DC Modifier |
|---|---|
| Per day since the event (max +10) | +2 |
| Scene is outdoors | +5 |
| Scene slightly disturbed | +2 |
| Scene moderately disturbed | +4 |
| Scene extremely disturbed | +6 |

**Collecting evidence** (preparing samples for a lab or other off-site analysis): DC 15. With success, the sample is usable; failure by 1–4 imposes a −5 penalty on the analyser's later check; failure by 5+ ruins the sample. Success by 10+ grants a +2 circumstance bonus to the later analyser.

*Collecting evidence requires an evidence kit; without one, take −4 on the check.*

### Search Hidden Areas *(d20 Modern Search; absorbed into Perception)*

The d20 Modern SRD text in the local vault does not contain a standalone Search section, but Pathfinder Perception explicitly covers searching for concealed doors, secret doors, and hidden traps via the general DC table above. Treat any d20 Modern reference to "Search" as Perception with the relevant DC from the General DC Table.

## Action

- Most Perception checks are **reactive** (made in response to a stimulus); no action is required.
- Actively searching for stimulus is a **move action**.
- Analysing a clue is a **full-round action**.
- Collecting evidence takes **1d4 minutes per object**.

---

## Try Again

Yes. You can attempt to notice something you previously missed if the stimulus is still present. For *active* attempts (move-action searches), the GM may rule that nothing further can be learned without new context. For *evidence collection*, samples cannot be re-collected unless more of the material exists at the scene.

---

## Special

- You can take 10 or take 20 on Perception checks (the d20 Modern Spot rule preserved — Pathfinder also allows this by default).
- A **sleeping character** can still make Perception checks, but takes a **−10 penalty** (d20 Modern carry-over preserved as a useful clarification).

### Feats granting Perception bonuses

- **Alertness** *(Pathfinder)*: +2 on Perception (and Sense Motive) checks.
- **Attentive** *(d20 Modern, applies to investigative use)*: +2 on Perception checks specifically when analysing clues / collecting evidence (formerly the Investigate skill).

### Racial & creature bonuses

- Elves, half-elves, gnomes, and halflings receive a **+2 racial bonus** on Perception.
- Creatures with the **scent** special quality: **+8** on Perception checks to detect a scent.
- Creatures with **tremorsense**: **+8** against creatures touching the ground.
- A spellcaster with a hawk or owl familiar: **+3** on Perception.

## Synergy

*The d20 Modern Knowledge (Behavioral Sciences) synergy that fed into Diplomacy now feeds into Sense Motive instead per the conversion table; it does not affect Perception.*

No specific synergy bonuses from other skills.

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/perception](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/perception/)):** Backbone of the file — General DC Table, Modifiers, racial/feat bonuses, take-10/take-20 default.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 77–82):** Audio-Specific DCs (Listen), Investigate-a-Scene rules (Analyse Clue + Collect Evidence), sleeping-character −10 penalty, Attentive feat. The Search skill is *not* defined in the local modernsrd.pdf — its function is fully absorbed into the Pathfinder Perception general table.
- **Naruto D20:** No existing vault file for Spot/Listen/Search/Investigate; no Naruto-specific content to merge. *(If a Naruto D20 supplement adds chakra-sensor mechanics later, they belong in [Chakra Control](Chakra%20Control.md) — see "Conceal Chakra Cost" — not in Perception.)*

**Conflict resolution:** None at the rule level. The Pathfinder unified-skill model wins over the d20 Modern four-separate-skills model; the d20M audio table and the Investigate analysis rules are preserved as *sub-sections* under Perception rather than as separate skills.
