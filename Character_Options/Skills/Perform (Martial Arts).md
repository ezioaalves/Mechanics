# Perform (Martial Arts)

**Ability:** Charisma (Cha)
**Trained Only:** No
**Armor Check Penalty:** No

Perform (Martial Arts) is the **Naruto D20 native** subcategory of Perform — the discipline of turning combat forms into spectacle. War-dances, weapon katas, public demonstrations, the stylised exhibition bouts that open clan festivals and intimidate rival delegations. It uses the generic Perform mechanics in [Perform](Perform.md); this file only adds what is specific to the Martial Arts subcategory.

---

## Description

A Perform (Martial Arts) check measures the technical and aesthetic quality of an exhibition fight, kata, or weapon demonstration. The check is judged for **display**, not for combat effectiveness — a flawless kata that strikes nothing is still a high Perform (Martial Arts) result.

In Kanigakure, Perform (Martial Arts) is the skill that backs *Yato weapons-shows on the Iron Docks*, *Garou ritual war-dances*, *Sendo opening ceremonies for new towers*, and the *Kaguya-clan bone-blade demonstrations* that double as recruitment for the academy.

---

## Check

Use the [generic Performance Quality DCs](Perform.md#performance-quality-dcs-pathfinder) — DC 10/15/20/25/30 for routine/enjoyable/great/memorable/masterful.

### Weapon and Style *(Naruto D20)*

The check is keyed to the form you are performing. If you switch styles or weapons mid-performance, each new style requires its own check (audiences notice transitions).

- **Armed performance** with a weapon you are **not proficient in:** −4 penalty. (Treat improvised weapons the same.)
- **Unarmed performance** without the **Combat Martial Arts** feat: −4 penalty.
- **Unarmed** with **Combat Martial Arts** OR **armed** with proficiency in the weapon: no penalty.

### Display Intent *(Kaihou house use)*

Choose the audience effect when declaring the performance. The DC table above sets the *quality floor*; the intent below describes what a Great-or-better performance can *do*:

| Intent | What a Great (DC 20) or better result achieves |
|---|---|
| **Showcase** | Build the performer's reputation. Treat a Great result as a +2 circumstance bonus to the next Diplomacy or Intimidate check against the same audience within the same scene; Memorable/Masterful results scale to +4/+6. |
| **Intimidate-via-display** | Substitute the Perform (Martial Arts) check for [Intimidate](Intimidate.md) against the audience for the purpose of demoralising opponents who watched the display. The −4 cap on demoralising larger groups (PF Intimidate) still applies. |
| **Recruit / Inspire** | Treat a Great result as the equivalent of a successful Diplomacy "make a request" check (one step of attitude improvement) against the audience as a whole, capped at Helpful only with Memorable or better. |

### Identifying the Style *(Kaihou house use)*

Observers can attempt to **identify the school or clan a Martial Arts performance derives from** with a Perform (Martial Arts) or [Knowledge (Arcana)](Knowledge%20(Arcana).md) check — DC 15 for common styles, DC 20 for clan-restricted forms, DC 25+ for Hijutsu-bearing forms. A successful identification reveals the school and one defining technique tag (e.g., "Hyuuga-derived Gentle Fist — chakra-network targeting").

## Action

A Perform (Martial Arts) display takes at least one full round (a quick kata flourish) up to **several minutes** for a complete exhibition bout or ceremonial war-dance. The generic Perform action-time guidance applies — a discrete performance, not a continuous activity.

---

## Try Again

Allowed. If the performance was witnessed by the same audience and failed (or merely was "routine"), subsequent attempts within the same scene take a **+2 DC increase per prior failure** (PF Perform standard).

---

## Special

- You can **take 10** on a Perform (Martial Arts) check in non-stressful conditions. You **cannot take 20**.
- The generic Perform mechanics — masterwork instrument bonus, Creative feat bonus, audience prejudice — apply where they make sense. A **masterwork weapon** grants the same **+2 circumstance bonus** that a masterwork instrument grants to Perform (Music). The **Creative feat** can be designated for Perform (Martial Arts) as one of its two subcategories.
- A character without an appropriate weapon (for an armed style that requires one) **automatically fails** the check, by analogy with Perform (Music) requiring an instrument. Empty-handed substitution is allowed if the performer has **Combat Martial Arts** — treat as an unarmed performance with no penalty.

---

## Synergy

- **Half base attack bonus as synergy bonus:** A performer adds **one-half their base attack bonus (rounded down)** as a synergy bonus to Perform (Martial Arts) checks. *(Naruto D20 native.)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in **[Knowledge (History)](Knowledge%20(History).md)** (formerly Knowledge (Art) per the conversion table) gains a **+2 bonus** on Perform (Martial Arts) checks — inherited from the parent skill. *(Naruto D20; rank threshold converted via the −3 rule.)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in [Taijutsu](Taijutsu.md) gains a **+2 bonus** on Perform (Martial Arts) checks — the skill that knows the technique is the skill that demonstrates it well. *(Kaihou house addition, mirroring the PF Knowledge → Perform synergy pattern.)*

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Naruto D20 (NarutoD20 Redux PDF, "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" → PERFORM (MARTIAL ARTS) (CHA)):** Canonical baseline. The skill is a Naruto D20 native addition to the Perform list; the −4 penalty for non-proficient weapon / no-Combat-Martial-Arts unarmed and the half-BAB synergy come straight from the PDF.
- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/perform](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/perform/)):** Performance quality DC table, masterwork-instrument analogue, take-10-yes / take-20-no, +2-DC-per-failure-when-retried.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 79):** Creative feat applicability, the no-instrument-auto-fail rule (extended here to no-appropriate-weapon by analogy).
- **Kaihou house additions:** The Display Intent table (Showcase / Intimidate-via-display / Recruit-Inspire), the Identifying-the-Style use, and the Taijutsu-synergy bonus are Kaihou extensions. They are flagged as "house" so they can be removed cleanly if the GM later wants strict Naruto D20 / PF behavior.

**Conflict resolution:** Naruto D20 wins on the core mechanic (the half-BAB synergy and the −4 penalty); PF supplies the DC table and the action/retry conventions; d20M's instrument-substitute rule is extended to weapons. No rule was discarded.
