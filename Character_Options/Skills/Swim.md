# Swim

**Ability:** Strength (Str)
**Trained Only:** No
**Armor Check Penalty:** Yes

Swim is the skill of moving through water under one's own power. As with Climb, water-walking is a Chakra Control effect rather than a Swim use — Swim governs *swimming proper*.

---

## Description

A successful Swim check lets you swim a portion of your speed through water. The water's condition sets the DC; your encumbrance and any armour matter both for the check itself (via Armor Check Penalty) and for what happens when you go under.

---

## Check

### Water Condition DCs

| Water | DC |
|---|---|
| Calm water | 10 |
| Rough water | 15 |
| Stormy water | 20 (you cannot take 10 in stormy water) |

### Movement

- **Move action:** Swim at **¼ your speed**.
- **Full-round action:** Swim at **½ your speed**.

### Failure

- **Fail by 1–4:** No progress this round.
- **Fail by 5+:** You go underwater.

### Underwater & Holding Your Breath *(Pathfinder formulation wins)*

You can hold your breath for **a number of rounds equal to twice your Constitution score**, provided you do nothing but take **move actions or free actions**.

If you take a **standard or full-round action** during this time, your remaining breath drops by 1 round per such action (effectively halving your breath-holding time during active combat).

After your breath-holding time runs out:
- Make a **DC 10 Constitution check** every round to continue holding your breath.
- The DC **increases by 1** each subsequent round.
- Failure means you begin to drown.

> *d20 Modern's version was Con score (not 2× Con) for breath-holding — Pathfinder's more generous formula wins per priority.*

### Fatigue from Long Swims

Each hour you swim, make a **DC 20 Swim check**. Failure deals **1d6 nonlethal damage** from fatigue. Repeated failures while already fatigued can drive the swimmer to exhausted and then unconscious (and, since unconscious characters sink, immediately into drowning).

---

## Action

A Swim check is either a move action (¼ speed) or a full-round action (½ speed) as described above.

---

## Try Again

A new check is allowed the round after a failed check.

---

## Special

- You can **take 10** on a Swim check (except in stormy water). You **cannot take 20**.
- You take **−1 on the Swim check per 5 pounds of gear** you carry, including armour and weapons (d20 Modern preserved — this is in addition to the Armor Check Penalty).
- A creature with a **swim speed** gains **+8 racial bonus** on Swim checks, can always take 10 (even in stormy water), and can run while swimming in a straight line.
- A character with the **Athletic** feat (Pathfinder) gains **+2** on Swim; **+4 at 10+ ranks** in Swim. *(Pathfinder threshold — no −3 conversion.)*

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/swim](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/swim/)):** Backbone — water-condition DCs, breath-holding at 2× Con score, fatigue rule, Athletic feat scaling at 10+ ranks, swim-speed +8 racial bonus.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 82):** Movement-per-action speeds (matches PF), gear penalty (−1 per 5 lbs), failure consequences, drowning escalation. The d20M breath-holding formula (Con score, not 2× Con) is **superseded** by Pathfinder's more generous version per priority.
- **Naruto D20:** No existing vault file for Swim and no entry in the NarutoD20 Redux "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section — Naruto D20 uses the unmodified d20 Modern Swim rules. No Naruto-specific content to merge.

**Conflict resolution:** The only meaningful conflict is breath-holding duration (PF: 2× Con; d20M: Con). Pathfinder's value wins per priority. Everything else is consistent.
