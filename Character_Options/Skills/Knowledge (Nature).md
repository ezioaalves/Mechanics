# Knowledge (Nature)

**Ability:** Intelligence (Int)
**Trained Only:** No (untrained checks limited to DC 10)
**Armor Check Penalty:** No

Knowledge (Nature) covers animals, plants, weather, seasons, vermin, fey, monstrous humanoids, and the forensic-medical knowledge of how living things work and stop working. Per the [Skill Conversion Table](../../House_Rules/Skill%20-%20Conversion.md), it absorbs the d20 Modern skill **Knowledge (Earth and Life Sciences)** — which Naruto D20 modified in the "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section to add the **Autopsy** mechanic.

---

## Description

A shinobi with Knowledge (Nature) can identify a plant, predict the weather, recognise an animal track, and read what killed a body. The skill is the natural-science companion to [Knowledge (Engineering)](Knowledge%20(Engineering).md) (the applied-sciences side) and [Knowledge (Arcana)](Knowledge%20(Arcana).md) (the chakra/occult side).

## Check

### General DCs *(Pathfinder)*

| Question | DC |
|---|---|
| Identify a common plant or animal | 10 |
| Identify an unnatural weather phenomenon (chakra-disrupted weather, summon-aura disturbance) | 15 |
| Identify a natural hazard (quicksand, poisonous spring, dangerous fungi) | 15 + hazard's CR |
| Determine that a natural feature is artificial in origin | 20 |
| Recognise an obscure species, region-specific plant, or unusual breeding | 25+ |

### Identify a Creature *(Pathfinder)*

- **DC = 10 + creature's CR** for animals, fey, monstrous humanoids, plants, and vermin.
- Each 5 points over the DC reveals an additional useful detail.

### Autopsy *(Naruto D20 — preserved verbatim from the source)*

You can study a dead body to determine its cause of death and uncover any secrets it still holds.

**Requires:** the **Forensics Training** feat. A character without a Disposal/Forensics Kit takes a **−4 penalty** on autopsy checks.

| Autopsy Goal | DC |
|---|---|
| Cause of Death | 15 |
| Examination (poisons/diseases in last 14 days) | 18 |
| Condition (HP, Chakra Pool, level, bloodlines, affinities) | 20 |
| Enhancements (shinobi drugs taken; components on further analysis) | 25 |
| Specialisation (one of: Str / Dex / Con score; or most-used technique type) | 28 |
| Necromancy (piece a broken body back together for further autopsy) | 30 |

**Cause of Death:** Determine the killing blow — mental trauma, poison, disease, chakra-coil damage, etc.

**Examination:** Determine poisons and diseases the subject was affected by in the **last 14 days** before dying, plus **1 day per point** you exceed the DC.

**Condition:** Determine the subject's maximum HP, Chakra Pool, level, advanced bloodlines, and elemental affinities at time of death.

**Enhancements:** Determine whether the subject had taken shinobi drugs and which; further analysis (GM discretion) may reveal the components used.

**Specialisation:** Determine one of: the subject's Str / Dex / Con score before death (excluding enhancement bonuses), OR the technique type the subject used the most (genjutsu, or — if ninjutsu/taijutsu — the favoured subtype if any).

**Necromancy:** Piece a broken body back together as long as you have all parts. After piecing, you can perform autopsy on it for **1 hour, plus 1 hour for every 5 points by which you beat the DC**. Necromancy can only be tried **once per corpse**.

**Aid:** Characters with ranks in **[Heal](Heal.md)** or **Knowledge (Nature)** can **aid** an autopsy even without the Forensics Training feat.

**Time & limits:** An autopsy attempt requires **1 hour**. You can perform **one autopsy per point of the corpse's Constitution modifier (while alive)** before the corpse becomes useless.

## Action

- General Knowledge recall: no action.
- Identifying a creature: no action.
- Autopsy: 1 hour per attempt.

---

## Try Again

- General recall: no.
- Autopsy: yes, up to the per-corpse limit (Con-mod attempts).

---

## Special

- **Untrained** Knowledge (Nature) checks are capped at DC 10.
- You can take 10 on Knowledge (Nature). You cannot take 20 unless on an autopsy with leisure time.
- A character with the **Educated** feat (d20 Modern) gains **+2** on any two Knowledge subcategories.
- The **Forensics Training** feat is required for Autopsy.

## Synergy

*(Naruto D20 / d20 Modern synergy thresholds converted via the −3 rank-prereq rule. Original values were all "5 ranks in Knowledge (Earth and Life Sciences)".)*

- A character with **2 or more ranks** in Knowledge (Nature) gains a **+2 bonus** on **[Craft (Pharmaceutical)](Craft%20(Pharmaceutical).md)** checks. *(Naruto D20 — preserved as Craft (Pharmaceutical); Kaihou rejects the Pathfinder "Alchemy" consolidation.)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in Knowledge (Nature) gains a **+2 bonus** on **[Perception](Perception.md)** checks made to investigate a scene (the Investigate sub-use of Perception). *(Naruto D20 — formerly "Knowledge (E&L Sciences) → Investigate".)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in Knowledge (Nature) gains a **+2 bonus** on **[Heal](Heal.md)** checks. *(Naruto D20 — formerly "Knowledge (E&L Sciences) → Treat Injury".)*

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Naruto D20 (NarutoD20 Redux v1.3.pdf "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section, p. ~38):** The Autopsy mechanic (entire sub-skill — Cause of Death, Examination, Condition, Enhancements, Specialisation, Necromancy, aid rule, time/limits). Cross-skill synergies preserved with the −3 rank conversion and re-mapped skill names per the conversion table (Craft (Pharmaceutical) → Craft (Alchemy); Investigate → Perception; Treat Injury → Heal).
- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/knowledge](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/knowledge/)):** Backbone — the topical scope (animals, plants, weather, etc.), the standard DC framework, the creature-identification formula.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 76 — Knowledge subcategories):** The Earth and Life Sciences topical scope (biology, botany, genetics, geology, paleontology, medicine, forensics) absorbed wholesale into Pathfinder's Knowledge (Nature).

**Conflict resolution:** Naruto D20's Autopsy use is **unique to this system** — preserved verbatim. The d20 Modern Knowledge (Earth and Life Sciences) topic scope (biology, botany, etc.) is broader than PF's Knowledge (Nature) by including some forensic/medical material; that breadth is preserved since the Autopsy use depends on it.
