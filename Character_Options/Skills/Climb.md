# Climb

**Ability:** Strength (Str)
**Trained Only:** No
**Armor Check Penalty:** Yes

Climb is the skill of ascending, descending, or traversing vertical and steep surfaces. Tree-walking and water-walking are Chakra Control effects, not Climb — but on any *physical* surface that resists gravity, this is the skill.

---

## Description

A successful Climb check lets you move up, down, or across a slope, wall, or other steep incline — even across a ceiling, if it has handholds — at **one-quarter your normal speed**. Failure by 1–4 means no progress; failure by 5+ means you fall from your current height.

---

## Check

### Climb DCs by Surface

| DC | Surface |
|---|---|
| 0 | Steep slope; knotted rope braced against a wall |
| 5 | Rope with a wall to brace against; knotted rope |
| 10 | Surface with ledges (rough wall, ship rigging) |
| 15 | Adequate handholds and footholds (rough stone, tree, unknotted rope, pitons in a wall) |
| 20 | Uneven surface with narrow holds (typical dungeon wall) |
| 25 | Rough natural rock or brick wall |
| 30 | Overhang or ceiling with handholds but no footholds |
| — | A perfectly smooth, flat vertical surface (impossible to climb) |

### Modifiers

| Condition | DC Modifier |
|---|---|
| Chimney (bracing against two opposite walls) | −10 |
| Corner (bracing against two perpendicular walls) | −5 |
| Slippery surface | +5 |
| Climb is less than 10 ft. *(d20 Modern preserved)* | −5 |

### Accelerated Climbing

You can climb at half your speed (instead of quarter) by accepting a **−5 penalty** on the Climb check.

### Catching Yourself While Falling

- **DC = wall's DC + 20** to catch yourself on a wall while falling.
- **DC = slope's DC + 10** to catch yourself on a slope.

### Damage While Climbing

Any time you take damage while climbing, make a Climb check against the surface's DC. Failure means you fall from your current height.

### Making Handholds *(d20 Modern preserved)*

You can drive **pitons** into a wall to create handholds: **1 minute per piton; one piton per 3 feet**. A wall with pitons has DC 15. An ice axe or similar implement can cut handholds in ice for the same effect.

---

## Action

Climbing is part of movement, so a Climb check is generally part of a move action. Each separate climb attempt during your turn requires its own check.

---

## Try Again

Yes, but a failure by 5+ means you've already fallen — you must climb back up before any new attempt at the position above the fall.

---

## Special

- You can **take 10** while climbing. You **cannot take 20**.
- While climbing, you **lose your Dexterity bonus to AC** and **cannot use a shield**.
- Climbing requires **both hands free** in general (one hand free if the GM allows).
- A creature with a **climb speed** gains a **+8 racial bonus** on Climb checks.
- A character with the **Athletic** feat (Pathfinder) gains **+2** on Climb; **+4 at 10+ ranks** in Climb. *(Pathfinder threshold — no −3 conversion.)*
- Without climbing gear, a character takes **−4** on Climb checks (d20 Modern; PF expresses this differently but the effect carries over). GM may waive for casual climbs.
- Someone using a rope can haul a character upward at **2× their maximum load** capacity (d20 Modern).

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/climb](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/climb/)):** Backbone — DC table by surface, accelerated climbing, catching-yourself formula, Athletic feat scaling at 10+ ranks, climb-speed +8 racial bonus.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 63):** "Climb less than 10 ft. reduces DC by 5" clarification, the pitons-and-handholds rule, the no-climbing-gear −4 penalty, the rope-haul 2× max load rule. All preserved as additions to Pathfinder (no conflict).
- **Naruto D20:** No existing vault file for Climb; no Naruto-specific content to merge.

**Conflict resolution:** Pathfinder and d20 Modern Climb are extremely similar in structure. PF wins where they differ (e.g. exact DC values on the surface table), and d20M-specific clarifications (the short-climb DC reduction, the pitons rule, the climbing-gear penalty) are preserved as additions.
