# Ride

**Ability:** Dexterity (Dex)
**Trained Only:** No
**Armor Check Penalty:** Yes

Ride is the skill of staying on a mount, controlling it in combat, and performing trick-riding manoeuvres. In Kaihou it absorbs the d20 Modern skill **Drive** (vehicles) per the [Skill Conversion Table](../../House_Rules/Skill%20-%20Conversion.md) — the same skill governs both animal mounts and operated vehicles in this campaign.

*Naruto D20 has no entry for this skill in the "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section — the unmodified Pathfinder rules apply, with d20 Modern's Drive notes preserved for vehicle use.*

---

## Description

A character with Ride saddles, mounts, dismounts, and controls an animal mount with skill. Most everyday riding doesn't require checks; combat, trick riding, and difficult conditions do.

For **vehicles** (carts, boats, modern conveyances if relevant): the same skill applies. d20 Modern's Drive rules are preserved below for vehicle-specific situations.

---

## Check

### Ride Tasks *(Pathfinder + d20 Modern Ride)*

| Task | DC |
|---|---|
| Guide with knees (free hands for combat or another action) | 5 |
| Stay in saddle (when the mount rears, bolts, or you take damage) | 5 |
| Fight while mounted (with a non-combat-trained mount) | 10 (PF) / 20 (d20M) — see note |
| Use mount for cover (drop down beside it for ½ cover, can't attack while doing so) | 15 |
| Soft fall (when you fall off; failure deals 1d6 falling damage) | 15 |
| Leap (over an obstacle while mounted; use lower of your Ride or mount's Acrobatics) | 15 |
| Spur mount (push for extra speed) | 15 |
| Fast mount or dismount (do it as a free action; failure makes it a move action) | 20 |
| Control mount in battle | 20 |

> **Fight-while-mounted DC note:** Pathfinder lists DC 10; d20 Modern lists DC 20. PF wins per priority. With a **combat-trained mount** (see [Handle Animal](Handle%20Animal.md) → Combat Riding), no check is needed — you use your move to direct the animal and your standard for your own attack.

### Animals Ill-Suited as Mounts

A creature not bred or trained as a mount imposes a **−2 penalty** on Ride checks involving it.

### Drive a Vehicle *(d20 Modern, preserved for setting versatility)*

Routine driving doesn't require a check. Make a Drive check when unusual circumstances apply (weather, ice, pursuit, time pressure) or for stunts.

- **Special vehicles** require the corresponding Surface Vehicle Operation feat. Without it, take **−4** on Drive checks.
- **Take 10** is allowed when driving; **take 20** is not.
- The **Vehicle Expert** feat grants **+2** on Drive checks.

> *In a primarily shinobi-flavoured campaign, the Drive use is rare. Foundry-District motorised vehicles, Iron-Docks boats, and the occasional cart use this side of the skill.*

---

## Action

- Most Ride checks are a **move action** integrated with movement.
- Some tasks (Cover, Soft Fall, Stay in Saddle) are **reactions** — no action cost.
- Fast Mount/Dismount, when successful, is a **free action**.

---

## Try Again

Most Ride checks have immediate consequences for failure (falling, losing control, taking damage) that make retrying impractical. Drive checks with non-fatal failure can usually be retried in subsequent rounds.

---

## Special

- **Bareback riding:** **−5 penalty** on all Ride checks (Pathfinder + d20 Modern).
- **Military saddle:** **+2 circumstance bonus** to Stay-in-Saddle checks (Pathfinder).
- A character with the **Animal Affinity** feat (Pathfinder & d20 Modern) gains **+2** on Ride; **+4 at 10+ ranks** in Ride (Pathfinder threshold — no −3 conversion).
- The **Vehicle Expert** feat (d20 Modern) grants +2 on Drive uses of this skill (does not stack with Animal Affinity, which applies to the mount side).

---

## Synergy

*(Naruto D20 / d20 Modern synergy thresholds converted via the −3 rank-prereq rule. Original value was "5 ranks in Handle Animal".)*

- A character with **2 or more ranks** in **[Handle Animal](Handle%20Animal.md)** gains a **+2 bonus** on Ride checks. *(Naruto D20.)*

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Naruto D20 (Ride (Dex).md, vault):** The Handle Animal → Ride synergy. Rank threshold "5" converted to **2** via the −3 rule. Naruto D20's PDF has no "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" entry for Ride.
- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/ride](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/ride/)):** Backbone — task DC table (Guide with Knees, Stay in Saddle, Fight Mounted, Cover, Soft Fall, Leap, Spur, Fast Mount/Dismount, Control), bareback penalty, military-saddle bonus, Animal Affinity scaling at 10+ ranks.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 70 — Drive; p. 80 — Ride):** Drive rules absorbed (routine vs unusual conditions, special-vehicle feat requirements, Vehicle Expert feat). Ride rules largely match Pathfinder; the higher DC (20) for fighting on a non-combat-trained mount is the one notable conflict — PF's DC 10 wins.

**Conflict resolution:** **Fight-while-mounted DC:** PF says 10, d20M says 20. PF wins. **Drive absorbed:** d20M Drive is a separate skill in that system; per the [Skill Conversion Table](../../House_Rules/Skill%20-%20Conversion.md), Drive maps to Ride in Kaihou. Both contexts (animal mounts and vehicles) are covered by this one skill.
