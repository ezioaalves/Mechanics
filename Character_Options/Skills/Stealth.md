# Stealth

**Ability:** Dexterity (Dex)
**Trained Only:** No
**Armor Check Penalty:** Yes

Stealth is the catch-all skill for moving and remaining unseen and unheard. In Kaihou it replaces the two separate d20 Modern skills **Hide** and **Move Silently**.

---

## Description

A character using Stealth is attempting to avoid being detected by senses — chiefly sight and sound. Where Perception asks "did the observer notice?", Stealth asks "did the actor stay hidden?". The two are opposed: every Stealth attempt is checked against the Perception of any creature in a position to notice.

---

## Check

Your Stealth check is **opposed by the Perception check** of anyone who might notice you. Creatures failing the opposed check treat you as having total concealment for the purpose of being aware of you.

### Movement Speed

| Speed | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Up to half normal speed | No penalty |
| More than half, up to normal speed | −5 |
| Running or charging | Stealth is impossible (−20 penalty equivalent in d20M; PF treats it as forbidden) |

### Size Modifiers

### Cover & Concealment *(d20 Modern Hide clarification — preserved)*

You cannot use Stealth without **at least one-half cover or one-half concealment** unless invisible. Greater cover or concealment grants circumstance bonuses on the Stealth check:

| Cover or Concealment | Circumstance Bonus |
|---|---|
| Three-quarters | +5 |
| Nine-tenths | +10 |

### Breaking Stealth

Your Stealth ends immediately after you make an **attack roll** (except when sniping — see below).

### Sniping

After using Stealth successfully at least **10 feet away**, you can make **one ranged attack** and then immediately use Stealth again **at a −20 penalty** to attempt to remain hidden after the shot.

### Creating a Diversion to Hide *(d20 Modern + Pathfinder both)*

Use a successful **Bluff** check to momentarily distract observers, then make a Stealth check while they look away. The Stealth check is at **−10 penalty** because you must move quickly; you also need to reach a hiding place within roughly 1 foot per rank in Stealth.

### Tailing in a Crowd *(d20 Modern Hide clarification — preserved)*

You can use Stealth to tail a person in public if there are other people around to mingle with. The GM may impose modifiers based on how careful the subject is being.

---

## Action

Stealth normally requires no separate action — it is integrated with movement. Sniping requires the ranged attack's action plus a Stealth check immediately afterwards.

---

## Try Again

Generally yes (every new round, every new movement, every new context). However, once an observer has noticed you, you must break line-of-sight and find concealment again before a fresh Stealth attempt can succeed.

---

## Special

- You can **take 10** on Stealth checks in non-stressful situations. You **cannot take 20** (Move Silently was take-10 only in d20 Modern; Pathfinder follows the same logic).
- **Invisibility** grants **+40 bonus** when immobile or **+20** when moving (Pathfinder).

### Feats granting Stealth bonuses

- **Stealthy** *(both Pathfinder and d20 Modern)*: +2 on Stealth checks (the d20 Modern version specifically buffed Move Silently +2; Kaihou unifies as +2 on Stealth).

---

## Synergy

No specific cross-skill synergies. Stealth interacts most often with Bluff (for Create-a-Diversion) and Perception (the opposing skill).

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/stealth](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/stealth/)):** Backbone of the file — single-skill model, size modifiers, sniping, invisibility bonuses, Stealthy feat.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 75 — Hide; p. 78 — Move Silently):** Cover & Concealment table (the −1/2 cover requirement, +5/+10 circumstance bonuses), Create-a-Diversion-to-Hide rules, Tailing-in-a-Crowd clarification, take-10-only restriction.
- **Naruto D20:** No existing vault file for Hide/Move Silently; no Naruto-specific content to merge.

**Conflict resolution:** The two d20 Modern skills (Hide for visual, Move Silently for audio) are unified into a single Pathfinder Stealth skill. Where the older rules made *different* opposed checks (Hide vs Spot for visual, Move Silently vs Listen for audio), Kaihou now uses one Stealth roll opposed by one Perception roll. The size modifier table from Hide is preserved (Move Silently had no size table). The Stealthy feat's +2 applies to *all* Stealth uses rather than just the audio half.
