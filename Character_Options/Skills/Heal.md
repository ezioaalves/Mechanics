# Heal

**Ability:** Wisdom (Wis)
**Trained Only:** No (most uses) / Yes (Surgery; Harvest/Implant Eye)
**Armor Check Penalty:** No

Heal is the medic's skill — first aid, long-term care, treating poison and disease, performing surgery, and (in Kaihou) the **forbidden art of harvesting and implanting doujutsu bloodline eyes**. Per the [Skill Conversion Table](../../House_Rules/Skill%20-%20Conversion.md), Heal is the Pathfinder name for the d20 Modern skill **Treat Injury**, which is the form the Naruto D20 PDF actually uses.

*Naruto D20 modifies this skill in the "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section (p. 40) with the **eye-harvest and eye-implant mechanics** — the canonical way to acquire a Sharingan, Byakugan, or other doujutsu bloodline through transplantation.*

---

## Description

A character with Heal stabilises the dying, drags a teammate back from negative hit points with a first-aid kit, treats poison and disease, and — given the Surgery feat — performs operations to restore hit points more effectively. With the right training and equipment, the same skill allows the **Mibu medical ninja** to harvest a fallen Uchiha's Sharingan and implant it into another shinobi, the exact pipeline by which Kakashi-style or Danzou-style bloodline transplants happen in canon.

## Check

### First Aid (Stabilise Dying) *(Pathfinder)*

- **DC 15.** As a standard action, stabilise a dying creature (negative hp).
- Equipment: first aid kit or medical kit (without one, **−4** penalty).

### Long-Term Care *(Pathfinder + d20 Modern)*

- **DC 15.** Day-long check; medical kit required.
- On success, patient recovers hit points and ability damage at **3× the normal rate** that day.
- One healer can tend **up to 6 patients** per day (PF) — Kaihou cap at one patient per rank in Heal for ND20 compatibility, **whichever is greater**.
- Healer cannot apply long-term care to self.

### Treat Deadly Wounds / Restore Hit Points *(Pathfinder, baseline)*

- **DC 20** (PF) or **DC 15** (d20M — Kaihou uses the **PF DC** as the canonical, since it's the more recent ruleset).
- Restores **1d4 hit points** as a full-round action with a healer's kit; **+1 per Heal rank above the DC** if you have the kit's full charges available.
- **Once per patient per day.**

### Revive Dazed / Stunned / Unconscious *(d20 Modern, absorbed)*

- **DC 15.** Attack action; first aid kit required.
- Removes the dazed, stunned, or unconscious condition (cannot revive a character at −1 hp or lower without stabilising first).

### Treat Poison *(Pathfinder + d20 Modern)*

- **DC = the poison's save DC.** Made as the patient's saving throw; on success, the healer's check result replaces the poison's save bonus that round.
- Standard action; 1 attempt per save the patient rolls.

### Treat Disease *(Pathfinder + d20 Modern)*

- **DC = the disease's save DC.** 10 minutes of care; medical kit required.
- On success, the patient gains a bonus on disease saves equal to the healer's Heal ranks for that 24-hour period.

### Surgery *(d20 Modern — requires the Surgery feat)*

- **DC 20** with a surgery kit.
- **−4 penalty** without the Surgery feat (the feat negates the penalty).
- Time: **1d4 hours**, +1 hour per negative-hp point the patient is below 0.
- On success, restores **1d6 hp per character level** of the patient (capped at full hp).
- Patient is **fatigued for 24 hours** after surgery (−2 hours per point the healer beat the DC; minimum 6 hours of fatigue).
- **Once per 24-hour period** per patient.

### Harvest Eye *(Naruto D20 — forbidden / restricted)*

> *Requires the **Surgery** feat. Cannot take 10 or 20. Without a medical kit or chakra scalpel, take **−4** on the check.*

- **Base DC 10**, modified by the conditions table below.
- Two **consecutive successful checks** required.
- Each check deals **1d4 damage** to the subject.
- **Failing two consecutive checks destroys the eye.**
- Subject must be **immobilised, paralysed, sedated, unconscious, or dead.**
- If the body has been **dead more than 1 hour per point of (alive) Con bonus**, the eye is unusable.
- Once harvested, the eye **must be preserved within 1 day** or become useless.
- Once an eye is harvested (or destroyed):
  - Subject takes **2 negative levels for 24 hours** (no save).
  - Subject takes **2d6 Constitution damage** (Fort DC 20 halves) and becomes **exhausted.**
  - If subject survives, they suffer **Doujutsu Donor Drawbacks** (NarutoD20 Redux Ch. 10) until they recover their own eye or receive a replacement of the same bloodline.
- **Time per attempt:** 1 minute.

### Implant Eye *(Naruto D20 — forbidden / restricted)*

> *Requires the **Surgery** feat. Cannot take 10 or 20. Without a medical kit or chakra scalpel, take **−4** on the check.*

- **Base DC 15**, modified by the conditions table below.
- **Up to three attempts.** If the third fails, the eye is **destroyed** and the recipient takes **1d6 Constitution damage**.
- If the recipient already has a bloodline **other than** the one being implanted, **the eye is rejected**: 1 Con damage per day until the eye is removed or the recipient dies.
- If the eye is destroyed mid-rejection: **2d6 Con damage** (Fort DC 20 halves; minimum equal to the Con damage already suffered).
- **Time per attempt:** 10 minutes.

### Harvest / Implant Eye — Conditions Table *(Naruto D20)*

| Condition | DC Modifier |
|---|---|
| Subject is conscious and cooperative | **+5** |
| Subject is conscious and resisting | **+10** |
| Each hour the eye has decayed* | **+2** |
| Poor lighting | **+4** |
| Surgery performed outside | **+2** |
| Subject has unfamiliar anatomy | **+5** |
| Halve the required time per check | **+5** |
| *(Implant only)* Eye is from a **Doujutsu bloodline** | **+5** |
| *(Implant only)* You have learned **Medical Ninjutsu Rank 6+** | **−10** |

*\*An eye decays when in a dead body or harvested-but-unpreserved.*

## Action

Varies by use — listed in each section above.

## Try Again

- **Yes** for: restoring hit points (next day), reviving dazed/stunned/unconscious, stabilising the dying, surgery, harvest/implant eye (within the limits listed).
- **No** for: long-term care for that day (the day's result stands), treating disease, treating poison.

---

## Special

- You can **take 10** on Heal. You can **take 20** only on restoring hit points or reviving dazed/stunned/unconscious targets.
- **Cannot take 10 or 20** on Harvest Eye or Implant Eye.
- Long-term care, restoring hp, treating disease or poison, and stabilising require a **medical kit**.
- Reviving requires a **first aid kit** or medical kit.
- Surgery requires a **surgery kit**.
- Harvest/Implant Eye requires a **medical kit** or **chakra scalpel** (the chakra-scalpel option is the medical-ninjutsu standard).
- Without the appropriate kit: **−4** penalty.
- Self-treatment is **limited to first aid, treat disease, treat poison**, and takes a **−5** penalty.
- The **Medical Expert** feat (d20 Modern) grants **+2** on all Heal checks.
- The **Healing Knack** Dedicated Hero talent (d20 Modern) grants **+2** on Heal checks.
- The **Healing Touch 1 / 2** talents stack additional restored hp on first-aid and surgery uses.
- The **Surgery** feat is **required** for Surgery, Harvest Eye, and Implant Eye uses.

## Synergy

*(Naruto D20 / d20 Modern synergy thresholds converted via the −3 rank-prereq rule. Original "5 ranks" → **2 ranks**.)*

- A character with **2 or more ranks** in **[Craft (Pharmaceutical)](Craft%20(Pharmaceutical).md)** (formerly Craft (Pharmaceutical) in d20M) gains a **+2 bonus** on Heal checks. *(Naruto D20.)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in **[Knowledge (Nature)](Knowledge%20(Nature).md)** (formerly Knowledge (Earth and Life Sciences)) gains a **+2 bonus** on Heal checks involving biological subjects (most uses). *(Naruto D20.)*
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in Heal gains a **+2 bonus** on **[Craft (Pharmaceutical)](Craft%20(Pharmaceutical).md)** checks (Treat Injury → Craft (Pharmaceutical) is in the Naruto D20 synergy list). *(Naruto D20.)*

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Naruto D20 (NarutoD20 Redux v1.3.pdf p. 40 — "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS"):** Harvest Eye and Implant Eye mechanics, the conditions table, the Doujutsu Donor Drawbacks consequence, the Medical Ninjutsu Rank 6+ modifier. **Preserved verbatim**, with the canonical skill name swapped from "Treat Injury (Wis)" to "Heal" per the conversion table.
- **Naruto D20 (synergy lists, vault):** Craft (Pharmaceutical) ↔ Heal and Knowledge (Nature) → Heal synergies. Rank threshold "5" converted to **2** via the −3 rule.
- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/heal](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/heal/)):** Backbone — DC 15 first-aid stabilisation, DC 20 treat deadly wounds for restoring hp, treat poison/disease per-save mechanics, the "1 patient per Heal rank" long-term care scaling.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 82 — Treat Injury):** First aid, long-term care, revive dazed/stunned/unconscious, restore hp, stabilise dying, surgery, treat poison, treat disease — the d20M version of every base use. The Surgery / Medical Expert / Healing Touch feats remain canonical for Kaihou via the d20M character-build chassis.

**Conflict resolution:** d20M Treat Injury and Pathfinder Heal are functionally the same skill with two different conventions for "Restore Hit Points" (PF: Treat Deadly Wounds DC 20, 1d4+extra; d20M: DC 15, 1d4). **Used PF's DC 20 as canonical** since PF is the second-tier ruleset and the higher DC matches the more-modern design. Surgery uses the d20M version (DC 20, 1d6 per character level) because Pathfinder folds surgical work into Treat Deadly Wounds in a less granular way — d20M's surgery rules are more useful for Kaihou's medical-ninja flavour. The NarutoD20 eye-harvest/implant rules are **untouched** — they are the marquee Naruto-specific use of the skill.
