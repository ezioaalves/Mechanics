# Appraise

**Ability:** Intelligence (Int)
**Trained Only:** No
**Armor Check Penalty:** No

Appraise is the skill of estimating the **Purchase DC** of an object — gear, art, antiques, captured loot, smuggled cargo, the contents of a fallen jounin's pouch. Per the [Skill Conversion Table](../../House_Rules/Skill%20-%20Conversion.md), Appraise has no d20 Modern row — that ruleset folded appraisal into the relevant Knowledge subcategory. **Kaihou keeps Appraise as a standalone Pathfinder skill** because the campaign uses Purchase DC pervasively and it's cleaner to let Appraise handle the valuation step without piggy-backing on whichever Knowledge happens to apply.

*Naruto D20 has no entry for this skill in the "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section (it does not exist as a standalone NarutoD20 skill) — base Pathfinder rules apply, modified to use Purchase DC instead of gold-piece values.*

---

## Description

A character with Appraise sizes up a piece of merchandise and names its **Purchase DC** within a degree of confidence — useful at the [Foundry District market](../../../Lore/World_of_Rokugan/Kanigakure/02%20-%20Foundry%20District/), the [Iron Wards](../../../Lore/World_of_Rokugan/Kanigakure/04%20-%20Iron%20Wards/) pawn-brokers, the Hoshigake-run smuggler's exchange in the Docks, or the inside of an enemy supply cache. The skill of any character who **buys, sells, or fences** for a living, and a useful adjunct for shopping-trip downtime scenes.

---

## Check

### Estimate Common Item *(Pathfinder, Kaihou Purchase-DC variant)*

- **DC 20** to determine the Purchase DC of a common item.
- On success: name the Purchase DC exactly (within a ±0 swing).
- On failure: the GM gives you a Purchase DC that is **1d2 higher or lower** (determine randomly).
- On failure by 5+: the GM gives you a Purchase DC that is **1d4 + 2 higher or lower**.

### Estimate Rare or Exotic Item *(Pathfinder)*

- **DC = 20 + the item's rarity modifier** (GM's call: +2 for uncommon, +5 for rare, +10 for masterwork or unique, +15 for legendary / forbidden / dou-jutsu-class).
- Examples: a ninja-tool of foreign manufacture (+2), a masterwork ninjato (+5), a Yato clan-pattern arm (+10), a captured Sharingan in preservation jelly (+15 — Kaihou-specific).
- On success: name the Purchase DC exactly.
- On failure: results scale with the rarity modifier (the rarer the item, the wider the miss).

### Detect Forgery / Counterfeit *(Pathfinder, with Kaihou flavour)*

- For obvious forgeries: **DC 20**.
- For skilled forgeries: **DC = the [Linguistics](Linguistics.md) check result** of the original forger (per Pathfinder's Linguistics Forge Document rules, which Kaihou's Linguistics skill inherits).
- On success, you identify the item as a forgery and (with a check that beats the DC by 5+) name the likely fabrication technique or workshop of origin.

> *In Kaihou: counterfeit ration tokens, forged courier seals, fake Hijutsu scrolls peddled to gullible genin, fraudulent Yato-clan-mark weapons. The Iron Wards keep busy with the bottom three.*

---

## Action

- Glance appraisal of a common item: **1 round** (move action with the item in hand).
- Detailed appraisal: **1 minute** per item.
- Bulk appraisal (a chest of loot, a smuggler's cache): GM call; typically **10 minutes** for a small haul, **1 hour** for a large one.

---

## Try Again

**No** on the same item by the same character (the check represents what you can tell from inspection). **Yes** with the assistance of a second character with the skill — make a fresh check; their result stands instead if higher.

---

## Special

- You can **take 10** on Appraise.
- You can **take 20** if you have **at least 1 hour** to inspect the item and access to comparison material (other items of similar type, market price-lists, a fence's reference book).
- Without **proper inspection tools** (a jeweller's loupe, a calligrapher's reading-glass for a scroll, a smith's gauge for a blade), take **−2** on Appraise checks for items where those tools meaningfully apply. The GM is the arbiter of *meaningfully*.

---

## Synergy

- A character with **2 or more ranks** in a relevant **Knowledge subcategory** gains a **+2 bonus** on Appraise checks for items of that subcategory's scope: **[Knowledge (Engineering)](Knowledge%20(Engineering).md)** for mechanical items and constructed weapons; **[Knowledge (Nature)](Knowledge%20(Nature).md)** for biological materials, pelts, herbal goods; **[Knowledge (History)](Knowledge%20(History).md)** for antiques and art objects; **[Knowledge (Religion)](Knowledge%20(Religion).md)** for relics and ritual paraphernalia; **[Knowledge (Arcana)](Knowledge%20(Arcana).md)** for chakra-infused or jutsu-related artefacts.
- A character with **2 or more ranks** in **[Craft](Craft%20(Weapons).md)** *(any subcategory)* gains a **+2 bonus** on Appraise checks for items within that Craft's scope (a Craft (Weapons) specialist sees craftsmanship a layperson misses).

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Naruto D20:** Does not exist as a standalone skill. The vault adopts Pathfinder's standalone Appraise rather than the d20M fold-into-Knowledge approach because Kaihou's economy runs on Purchase DC, which appraises cleanly with one shared skill instead of being scattered across every Knowledge subcategory.
- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/appraise](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/appraise/)):** Backbone — DC 20 baseline for common items, rarity modifiers for rare/exotic items, detect-forgery cross-link to the Linguistics skill (which holds the Forge Document rules).
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 76 — Knowledge):** No standalone Appraise skill. Appraisal is "one sort of task that can be performed using Knowledge", with the relevant Knowledge subcategory rolled instead. Kaihou supersedes this with the standalone Pathfinder approach, **but the d20M "use a Knowledge subcategory" rule survives as the synergy clause above** — meaning a character with the relevant Knowledge gets a +2 bonus stacked onto the Appraise roll, capturing the d20M intent without making players choose which skill to roll.

**Conflict resolution:** d20M's "Knowledge-as-Appraise" approach is converted to a synergy bonus rather than overriding the standalone Pathfinder skill. The Pathfinder gold-piece convention is replaced with Purchase DC throughout (Kaihou's universal economic convention).
