# Handle Animal

**Ability:** Charisma (Cha)
**Trained Only:** Yes
**Armor Check Penalty:** No

Handle Animal is the skill of commanding, training, and rearing animals — from a Dattoumaru war-dog to an Inuzuka ninken to the messenger crows of an Eiji-trained recon unit. *Naruto D20 has no entry for this skill in the "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" section — the unmodified Pathfinder rules apply, with d20 Modern preserved as a more detailed reference for general-purpose training.*

---

## Description

A character with Handle Animal directs animals to perform known tricks, pushes them past their normal limits, teaches new tricks, and trains them for general purposes (combat, guarding, riding, etc.). Druids and Rangers in standard d20 have an inherent animal-companion bond; shinobi summon contracts and clan animal bonds (Inuzuka ninken, Aburame insects, Eiji's crows) operate through different mechanics and don't necessarily require Handle Animal — but this skill governs *trained* animals that aren't bonded.

## Check

### Task DCs

| Task | DC | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Handle an animal | 10 | Move action |
| "Push" an animal (force it past its limits) | 25 | Full-round action |
| Teach an animal a trick | 15 or 20 (see Tricks) | 1 week |
| Train an animal for a general purpose | 15 or 20 (see Purposes) | See Purposes |
| Rear a wild animal from infancy | 15 + animal's HD | Months |

If the animal is wounded or has taken ability damage, **+5 to the DC** to handle or push it.

### Tricks *(Pathfinder list)*

An animal can know a limited number of tricks (typically Int score). Each trick is one teaching project.

**DC 15 tricks:** Bury · Come · Deliver · Down · Fetch · Guide · Heel · Intimidate · Perform · Seek · Serve · Sneak · Stay · Subdue · Warm · Watch · Work · Calm (DC 10).

**DC 20 tricks:** Aid · Attack · Break Out · Defend · Exclusive · Feint · Flank · Flee · Get Help · Guard · Hunt · Maneuver · Menace · Milk Venom · Protect · Quiet Watch · Rescue · Spin Silk · Track · Throw Rider · Withhold Venom.

**DC 25 tricks:** Build Simple Structure · Detect · Drive · Entertain · Hide Tracks · Mark Territory · Nest · Pose as Scenery · Receive Spell · Speak.

### General Purposes *(d20 Modern; an animal trained for a purpose knows a bundled set of tricks)*

| Purpose | DC | Training Time | Tricks Granted |
|---|---|---|---|
| Combat Riding | 20 | 6 weeks | Attack, Come, Defend, Down, Guard, Heel |
| Fighting | 20 | 3 weeks | Attack, Down, Stay |
| Guarding | 20 | 4 weeks | Attack, Defend, Down, Guard |
| Heavy Labour | 15 | 2 weeks | Come, Work |
| Hunting | 20 | 6 weeks | Attack, Down, Fetch, Heel, Seek, Track |
| Performance | 15 | 4 weeks | Come, Fetch, Heel, Perform, Stay |
| Riding | 15 | 3 weeks | Come, Heel, Stay |

An animal trained for one general purpose may be upgraded to another (e.g. Riding → Combat Riding) by spending 3 weeks and making a DC 20 Handle Animal check. Upgrading replaces previously-trained tricks outside the new purpose.

---

## Action

- **Handle:** move action.
- **Push:** full-round action.
- **Teach trick / train for purpose:** see the tables. Training requires **3 hours per day** for the listed number of consecutive days — more than 3 hours/day does not reduce the count, and missing a day wastes the effort.

---

## Try Again

Yes, except for **rearing** a wild animal — that's a one-shot attempt per animal.

---

## Special

- You can **take 10** or **take 20** when handling, pushing, or teaching/training in low-pressure conditions.
- An **untrained character** can use Charisma checks to handle and push *domestic* animals, but cannot teach or train them.
- A character with the **Animal Affinity** feat (d20 Modern) and at least 1 rank in Handle Animal gains **+2** on the skill.
- **Druids and Rangers** gain **+4** with their animal companions (Pathfinder). *(Limited relevance in Kaihou — shinobi don't typically take druid/ranger classes; the closest analogue is the Beastlord prestige class.)*

---

## Synergy

*(Naruto D20 / d20 Modern synergy thresholds converted via the −3 rank-prereq rule. Original value was "5 ranks".)*

- A character with **2 or more ranks** in Handle Animal gains a **+2 bonus** on **[Ride](Ride.md)** checks. *(Naruto D20 — knowing how to command an animal helps when you're on its back.)*

## Source Notes

*Priority: Naruto D20 > Pathfinder > d20 Modern.*

- **Naruto D20 (Handle Animal (Cha).md, vault):** The Handle-Animal-→-Ride synergy. Rank threshold "5" converted to **2** via the −3 rule. Naruto D20's PDF has no "NEW USES FOR EXISTING SKILLS" entry for Handle Animal — the base d20 Modern rules apply unmodified except for the synergy note.
- **Pathfinder ([d20pfsrd.com/skills/handle-animal](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/skills/handle-animal/)):** Backbone — task DC table, the comprehensive trick list (DC 15/20/25), the rear-wild-animal mechanic, the Druid/Ranger +4 with companion.
- **d20 Modern (modernsrd.pdf p. 74):** The General Purposes table (Combat Riding, Fighting, Guarding, Heavy Labour, Hunting, Performance, Riding — each with DCs, training time, and bundled tricks). Preserved as a practical alternative to teaching individual tricks one at a time. The Animal Affinity feat preserved.

**Conflict resolution:** No major conflict. PF's trick list is much richer than d20M's; d20M's "general purpose" bundles are not in PF but are useful at the table — preserved as a complementary teaching path. The Untrained-Charisma-for-domestic-animals clarification is from d20M; preserved.
