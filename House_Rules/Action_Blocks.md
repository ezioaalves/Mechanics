# Action Blocks

Downtime in Kanigakure is measured in **Action Blocks**. Every PC gets **3 blocks per day**. A block is roughly 4 hours of focused effort — half a day.

> **This page is a guideline, not a hard rule.** A single 4-hour block can fit several skill checks; the GM judges when an activity is heavy enough to consume more than one. The defaults below assume one block per activity unless the activity is explicitly larger (training, C-rank missions, long crafts).

## Default: 1 Action Block

Most downtime activities cost **1 block**:

- Influence check or Discovery check ([[Influência]])
- Gather Information / Sense Motive at a venue
- A single D-rank skill resolution
- Networking with a named NPC
- Browsing a market's open stock **and buying** items
- Light maintenance, repair, or self-care
- Travel inside the village
- One investigation step or one social scene

Rule of thumb: if the activity is a single check or a single short interaction, it costs 1 block. Many small checks can be chained inside the same 4-hour window when narratively reasonable — the GM decides when to charge a second block.

## Training: 2 Action Blocks

A full training day toward learning a technique costs **2 blocks** and drains ~80% chakra (per [[11_Learn DC]]). The leftover 1 block of a training day is still usable for one of the 1-block activities above — this is where most of the campaign's social and mission economy happens.

A second training session in the same day is not allowed: the chakra cost would deplete the PC.

## Crafting

Crafting time is computed under the relevant Craft rules (Purchase DC, materials, complexity), independently from the block grid. The total time is then **rounded up to whole Action Blocks** for scheduling purposes:

- 1–4 hours → 1 block
- 5–8 hours → 2 blocks
- 9–12 hours → 3 blocks (a full day)
- 13+ hours → spans multiple days; round up to whole blocks

A craft that fits within a single block can be combined with the rest of the day normally. Crafts longer than one block lock those blocks against other activity.

## Extended Activities

Some activities are inherently larger than one or two blocks. The GM frames these as "this fills your day" or "this fills the next two days," rather than itemizing them:

- A fully played C-rank mission (3 blocks for the day, often spills into the next day for travel/recovery)
- Multi-day infiltration prep
- Formal hearings, banquets, large clan obligations
- Deep research or scroll decryption

## Hard Limits

- A block can pay for only one activity at a time. Splitting a block is not allowed.
- A PC at 20% chakra or lower cannot start a 2-block training session.
- C-rank missions consume the entire day's 3 blocks and frequently the next day as well.

## Typical Day Patterns

- **Training day:** 2 blocks training + 1 block free (Influence, market, D-rank, light craft).
- **D-rank day:** 1 block per D-rank, mixed with social or market actions.
- **C-rank day:** all 3 blocks gone; no training, no shopping.
- **Recovery / social day:** 3 blocks of Influence, networking, shopping, downtime scenes.

## Designer Intent

Players will spend most days training (2 blocks). The leftover 1 block per day is the campaign's **social economy**: it pays for Influence checks ([[Influência]]), market visits, and Mission Board engagement. Every location card should size its actions against the 1-block default — anything heavier needs a reason.
